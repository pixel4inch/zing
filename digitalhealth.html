WHAT'S TRENDING

Over the last few years, there has been significant growth in the adoption of cutting edge technologies. More than 4.8 billion people across the world are active internet users and the increase in smartphone adoption has also contributed largely in expanding healthcare’s digital footprint. Today, several patients want their digital experience in healthcare to mimic their retail experience. Patients are more likely to choose healthcare services according to their digital competencies. Most of the patients prefer to use smartphones to connect with their healthcare providers. Also, there has been an expansion of personalized monitoring devices and mobile healthcare apps. Use of wearable devices related to health and fitness has increased significantly in the last few years and this increase is anticipated to continue in the future. Developments in digitalization, increasing interest among people in health & wellness, and enhanced capabilities of affordably-priced wearables are driving patient empowerment and connecting health-related data to mainstream consumer experience. Widespread availability of medical information allows patients to understand their health better, choose their health care providers, and play an active role in determining the necessary course of treatment, all these remotely without visiting a doctor. Accordingly, traditional healthcare models are undergoing changes to accommodate the more proactive role of the patients and to meet their demands for personalized and cost-effective treatments based on their personal preferences and medical history.


As technological advancements continue to proliferate the healthcare ecosystem, more patients and physicians are willing to use digital devices to connect with each other to receive and/or provide care. Over the next couple of years, several healthcare companies will become more digitalized and will migrate to more technological friendly way of operating. The rising wave of digital innovation around healthcare is supported by aging population, rise in infectious and chronic diseases and shortage of skilled healthcare professionals. Developing countries in particular, face a significant shortage of skilled clinicians to address the growing healthcare burden. The widening gap between professionals and increasing population is creating significant challenges for public and private health systems. India faces a significant shortage of medical professionals in rural areas where more than two thirds of the population reside. In Germany, there is expected to be a shortage of 1.3 million health care professionals by 2030. Healthcare providers are therefore turning to sophisticated tools such as data analytics and Internet-of-Things to recalibrate business models to meet rising consumer demand. Digital health is also driven by the rise and proliferation of patient data stored in the form of electronic forms saving time and space. Electronic health records (EHRs) or electronic medical records (EMRs) comprise data of patient prescription, drug intakes, and medical history enabling the physicians to commence accurate diagnosis and reduce waiting times.


Increasing use of tablets and smartphones by healthcare experts, the growing footprint of IT companies in the healthcare sector, and favorable government rules are some other major factors fueling market growth. Growing use of 4G/5G networks, cloud computing, wireless technology, and rise of wearable electronics are driving exponential increase in the scale and pace of the emergence of digital healthcare innovations. Disruptive technologies such as virtual and digital reality, artificial intelligence (AI), Cloud, RPA, Blockchain, robotics, and internet of medical things (IoMT) are helping diagnosis and treatment in terms of speed, accuracy, and quality, and enhancing overall patient experience. In addition, these advances are contributing to increased consumer expectations, which is replacing “going digital” with “being digital” as the new requirement for all healthcare organizations.


The prominence of healthcare analytics has been growing owing to several factors such as thirst for information by the stakeholders; need to manage huge, diverse data; growing competition; increasing regulatory complexity; and technological advancements in the medical field. Meaningful and robust analytics has the power to change a healthcare organization and place it ahead of its competitors. The need for efficient healthcare analytics becomes even more vital with the organizations operating in value-based care. Analytics platforms are expected to become the key competitive differentiators for the healthcare organizations that shift to value-based care. As the availability of data keeps growing from sources such as smart medical devices, electronic health claims, records and social media, insights driven by analytics are expected to play a vital role in helping the organizations to improve their quality and costs, offer better treatment to at-risk patients, and connect with wide range of consumers.


Tele-health is the most popular segment of mHealth applications and at the center of the burgeoning telehealth trend in the healthcare sector is its deliverability through smartphones that are the most used device in the world. Public reimbursement, prevailing regulations, strategy and policy landscape, along with necessary government support are the critical elements that currently define the future of telemedicine industry. Nations with matured telemedicine system in place, are likely to retain teleconsultations beyond COVID-19. Improved medical care at affordable rates can be achieved by integration of pharmaceutical companies with telemedicine services, thereby leading to higher gains. Analogously, MedTech industry can benefit too, by the integration of medical equipment with telemedicine services. Mobile devices and apps are finding an increasing role in telemedicine. Several telemedicine companies are introducing telemedicine apps to enable patients to access and communicate with healthcare providers in remote locations.


Growing use of 4G/5G networks and increased application areas support the growth of digital health. Major technological advances such as computing power, wireless technology, and miniaturization are driving an exponential increase in the scale and pace of the emergence of digital health care innovations, and impacting both business and clinical operations. In addition, these advances are contributing to increased consumer expectations, which is replacing “going digital” with “being digital” as the new requirement for all health care organizations. Innovations in digital health care are emerging in an increasingly greater scale and pace. Digital technologies provide support to the health systems in their efforts of transitioning to new patient-centered care models and help them adopt “smart health” approaches that would increase access and improve quality at lower costs. Disruptive technologies such as virtual and digital reality, artificial intelligence (AI), Cloud, RPA, Blockchain, robotics, and internet of medical things (IoMT) are helping diagnosis and treatment in terms of speed, accuracy, and quality, and enhancing overall patient experience. As health care becomes “shoppable” with a care-sharing model presenting increasing costs for patients, enhancing patient experience could be the potential area for significant change. Many non-traditional companies from the retail, technology, and consumer sectors are also starting to offer solutions that could disrupt the norm, with readiness to enter into the health care value chain.

As the healthcare landscape continues to evolve with each passing day, several high impact technologies are stealing the limelight for their ability to deliver better patient care at lower costs and improve lives. Discussed below are few of the important technology innovations that have the potential to transform your healthcare organization in 2023.


  • Artificial Intelligence – AI is transforming the concept of healthcare delivery. Healthcare AI applications are capable of augmenting human activity, i.e. take up tasks such as medical imaging, diagnosis of health conditions and risk analysis, which is a considerable progress from legacy technologies consisting of algorithms or tools that were capable of only complementing actions of humans. With healthcare organizations confronting challenges in the form of rising operating costs amidst increasing labor expenditure, growing demand for digital services, and rising need for interoperability, AI is emerging as a technology with the potential to address such challenges. The advent of smart systems, burgeoning data volumes and foray of new players are all aiding adoption of AI systems in the healthcare sector across varied areas including virtual assistants, diagnostics, wearables and wellness and lifestyle management. AI is being adopted in an expanding range of healthcare applications, from drug discovery and diagnostic systems to personalized treatment and patient care. Continuously changing healthcare delivery, payment and timeline options have created the need for innovative solutions for managing patient information and supporting decision making. Increasing focus on decision making and data mining options has also enabled AI-powered tools to lead the healthcare revolution. AI algorithms and software are being used to analyze medical data for predicting diseases and providing appropriate medication, which allows optimization and personalization for enhanced outcomes.
  • Blockchain - Blockchain technology also represents a paradigm and cultural shift of the healthcare industry toward enhanced collaboration and streamlined workflows. With compelling merits, the technology is forecast to find extensive adoption in the healthcare market and breach the $6 billion mark by 2025. Blockchain holds tremendous potential to enable high degree of interoperability, enhanced clinical data management and flawless supply chains. Blockchain presents the perfect technology for dealing with various challenges associated with time-intensive, manual options employed by the healthcare industry for, provider credentialing and patient record sharing. The technology eliminates issues caused by conventional data-sharing approaches and infrastructure, such as privacy concerns, duplicate records and false positives. Blockchain allows organizations to share patient data in an auditable manner while mitigating privacy issues. The technology allows sharing of information or transaction details in real-time in an immutable and secure fashion. In the pharmaceutical value chain for instance, blockchain technology impacts all aspects, including improving supply chain transparency, and enhancing data collection in clinical trials. With blockchain technology, the supply chain of drugs and medical equipment can become more secure, streamlined and efficient. The technology-backed development and maintenance of medical supply inventories at a global scale can provide help in addressing the medical supply shortages, prevent entry of counterfeit products into supply chain, and also resolve quality control related issues. Blockchain in clinical trials enables patients to gain complete control over the patient data. Integration of blockchain also enables clinicians to undertake secure sharing of clinical data.
  • Healthcare Cloud - Healthcare cloud computing model helps healthcare organizations to store and manage of their healthcare IT resources as services over cloud. By helping provide access to healthcare information systems and data hosted on external cloud servers to healthcare organizations over the Internet in shared/multi-tenant, dedicated or hybrid service models, healthcare cloud computing especially comes across as the most cost effective healthcare information system provisioning model for healthcare organizations. Collaboration and sharing is as one of the chief clinical benefits of cloud technology. Cloud technology enables different departments which traditionally have maintained huge volumes of data in isolated data silos to share it and in process make optimal utilization of stored data. Data portability and sharing can enable healthcare centers to innovate and extend remote diagnostic and healthcare services to even remote population centers. Easy collaboration centrally accessible data between clinical researchers, physicians and diagnostics can enable development of more effective drug delivery system that can help counter even serious diseases. Given the considerable costs and efforts involved in conducting security assessments, certifications and audits that are often required to demonstrate compliance with the HIPAA security policies, it forces tremendous burden on organizations operating on-premise infrastructure. Cloud technology on the other hand shifts the responsibility of such costs on the cloud service provider (CSP) and since they can bear such costs more easily when compared to private datacenters, cloud technology presents a cost effective option. Furthermore the deployment of an effectively designed and developed cloud based PHI solution can ease compliance technical as well as physical safeguards as stipulated by the HIPAA rules.
  • Healthcare Analytics – Healthcare analytics is increasingly used by healthcare organizations to identify new revenue opportunities and use cases. The healthcare analytics domain is comprised of diverse analytical techniques that include prescriptive analytics such as decision-related models to augment clinical priorities as well as financial outcomes; predictive analytics such as modeling and simulation techniques; and descriptive techniques such as reporting technologies and tools. Healthcare analytics is a vast subject the ambit of which spans diverse domains such as customer acquisition, clinical delivery, personalized medicine, and operational efficiency. While analytics holds potential to help organizations in generating more revenues, improving patient outcomes and reducing costs, the approach requires organizations to capture the real essence with appropriate strategies. The market anticipates significant demand in the upcoming years owing to the outbreak. However, constraints such as misuse of patient information and high capital disbursement and maintenance may hamper the market expansion in the short run. As businesses work to address these constraints and lower the cost of digital health solutions, product adoption rates will increase, boosting the market growth. As the role of data analytics increases in healthcare, the sector could benefit in some major ways, including digital health platforms, automated healthcare, data-driven clinical evidence, and AI- and IoT-enabled real-time patient care, practitioner performance evaluation and patient costs.
  • Internet of Things (IoT) - IoT in healthcare has the potential to provide substantial improvement in operational efficiencies by providing improved quality of care at a minimized cost. IoT can help staffing managers in providing for better staffing and scheduling efficiencies, thereby improving workflows. For instance, IoT helps in collecting data related to location of staff, patient location, and the location of important medical equipment. The data can be used to ascertain the movement of equipment and staff, and would enable in predicting the need for the same in various departments. In this way, hospitals will be able to make proper resource allocation and on-demand scheduling. IoT is also useful in handling the critical issue of alert fatigue, which is responsible for a growing number of patient deaths or injuries. Alert fatigue happens when there are too many false alarms. With the help of IoT, healthcare facilities can link smart monitoring devices with patient rooms, records, nursing staff, pharmacies, and other critical information. The sensors embedded in these smart devices capture and collect data, which is then analyzed to ascertain whether a loud alarm should be triggered for critical, life-threatening events or a silent alarm should be triggered for harmless, non-critical events. IoT will therefore help in reducing false alarms, cuts down on work load, and improves timely action, thereby augmenting patient safety and care. Besides improving operational efficiencies, IoT in healthcare also supports physicians in making informed, more accurate and faster diagnosis; and in deciding personalized treatment agendas.
  • Robotics - Robot-Assisted surgery (RAS), an advanced technological field utilizes robotic skills to aid a surgeon in undertaking precise and delicate procedures in the OR. RAS utilizes precision tools using computerized images and performs surgical procedures with greater accessibility and accuracy than a human hand. Though benefits of RAS are crystal clear, prohibitive costs continue to hinder large-scale adoption of this technology. However, factors such as increased success rates, shorter hospital stay, commercial interests as well as decline in patient morbidity are propelling the growth of RAS. Government focus, application in new surgical areas along with increased awareness among surgeons and healthcare professionals leading to increased adoption is expected to fuel the use of RAS, thereby enabling reduction in costs. Minimally invasive surgery was traditionally used for removal of gallbladder and prostate, as well as for gastrointestinal, gynecologic and urologic procedures. However, with robots being introduced in the medical sector, robot-assisted minimally invasive/non-invasive procedures are slowly replacing more conventional invasive procedures. Starting out as an aid to minimally invasive surgery (MIS), surgical robots have made the big leap and are now slowly taking centerstage in complex surgical procedures.
  • Augmented Reality - From healthcare professions to medical companies, the entire healthcare industry is opening up to the benefits of augmented reality to carry out their day to day activity. Product research and development teams at medical devices, drugs and pharmaceutical industries can use mobile augmented reality devices, including smart AR glasses to gain 3D view of their products in development or production for more information. Doctors and nurses too can benefit from being able to access critical patient information directly from their near eye devices. Specific apps allow smart AR glasses to overlay information of treatment area directly on the doctor’s field of view. These glasses also allow doctors to monitor patients' vital signs during surgical procedures without having to take their eyes off the patient. Healthcare professions can use smart AR glasses to collect multi-dimensional anatomical data of the patient from different sensors such as CT scans, Magnetic Resonance Imaging, and Computed Tomographic scans, which can be combined in real time with patient view. Live-streaming of surgeries, diagnostic imaging and patient care management are some of the applications driving adoption of AR. Implementing AR enabled smart glasses in healthcare systems offers key benefits such as error reduction, time savings, provides real-time assist in complicated surgeries that require precision, improved clinical documentation, improved patient experience, clinical outcomes and delivery of health care, increased face- to-face engagement with patients, facilitates collaboration in unusual medical cases, and improved education and training programs.

Similar to what’s being seen in most other industries, the healthcare industry is also evolving at lightning speeds. Dynamic technology changes are responsible for the rapid emergence of new trends that will open up not only new avenues for growth in the healthcare sector but speed up diagnosis, improve overall patient care and reduce costs. Listed below are few of the trends making waves in the industry.


  • mHealth Care Gains Momentum: mHealth care is the most prominent trend within the digital health care market. Of late, mobile health (mHealth) care apps are being increasingly leveraged for performing critical functions, such as patient-to-physician communication. The availability of these health care apps facilitates management of health care-related information, monitoring of patients, scheduling patient appointments, and management of electronic medical records. The contribution of mobile health care technologies within the health care industry is most profound, with long-ranging effect on the industry as a whole. In several regions of the world, in which the penetration of mHealth care apps into the overall health care systems is complete, these apps are making significant contribution in preventing, treating, and managing several chronic diseases. For example, of late, mHealth care apps are playing a key role in preventing and managing Type 2 diabetes. Quin is an app powered by machine learning and algorithmic insights, which aims to help people suffering from type 1 diabetes to decide the time and amount of insulin to be taken daily. Driven by the phenomenal increase in the availability of numerous fitness and mHealth care apps, the overall digital health care industry is experiencing exponential growth. Today, while the health care market is flooded with over 318,000 health care apps, 100 new mHealth care apps are being launched on a daily basis. While the increase in the number of health care apps is indeed phenomenal, more significant is their penetration and reach. Today, a minimum of one health care app is being used by a large population of smart phone users, and mHealth care apps are playing an important role within the overall health care ecosystem. The effect of health care apps is so era-defining and widespread that, health care facilities not adopting mHealth care apps run the risk of losing customers. The integration of mHealth care apps with latest technologies, including the Internet of Things (IoT) enabled connected health care devices, big data analytics, Blockchain, and geolocation technologies, among several others, is expected to decisively impact the future growth within the worldwide market for digital health apps.
  • Home Health Clubs Make it to the Spotlight - Home Health Hub is the revolutionary step forward in supporting continuity and care coordination between ACSs and primary healthcare systems. The technology is geared to support remote patient monitoring, tele-health, tele-medicine, and virtual healthcare delivery. Home Health Hub is defined as a combination of hardware and software systems that allow the creation of a medical hub for monitoring, acquiring, and transmitting patient data from point-of-care facility to healthcare professionals in the primary care sector. Home care agencies, nursing homes and assisted living facilities utilize a wide range of home healthcare technologies and represent “hubs” for collecting and transmitting patient data. They therefore are major end-users of home health hub products and services. However, rapid proliferation of digital health technologies are bringing healthcare even closer to the patient, making the patient’s home the point-of-care facility. In addition to the ubiquity of smartphones and tablets and the rise of smartphone-enabled home health hubs, growing penetration of smart homes is also expected to spur growth in the market. Smart homes are helping drive the trend towards connected home-based self-care. A smart home hitherto valued for its unrivalled convenience and comfort is now gaining popularity for its potential to enhance the home healthcare experience. They will play an increasingly important role in the evolution of digital, remote, connected and virtual care systems. A wide range of home monitoring devices, home-based sensors, consumer electronics and mobile apps will help smart homes evolve from just meeting aging-in-place, chronic disease management and post-acute care monitoring needs to healthcare needs of the entire family i.e. maternal, infant/child, adolescent health, and care for physically and intellectually disabled. Sharing electronic healthcare records, managing healthcare sensor data, cloud execution of multimedia-based health services, patient counseling and assisted-living services are few of the healthcare services supported by smart homes. While medical wearables and smartphones enable remote patient monitoring and telemedicine services targeted at disease management, smart homes can help expand the functionality of digital health services from just disease management to health management. In other words, IoHT and smart homes when combined with cloud will revolutionize digital health and will expand the role of digital health beyond elderly care and health monitoring to other areas of wellness, nutrition, exercise, sleep, medication monitoring, mental, social wellbeing, managing pregnancy and prenatal health, among others.
  • 2023 Will be Year of Medical Wearables - Wearable devices are taking a lead role in Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM), which consists of collecting health and medical data from a patient in a remote location. Remote health monitoring is growing in popularity as both patients and healthcare professionals want health to be monitored outside of clinical settings. Remote patient monitoring, also referred to as remote patient management, is a method of healthcare delivery that leverages the latest technological advances to gather patient data outside of traditional healthcare settings. RPM when incorporated in the management of chronic diseases, has the potential to significantly improve quality of life for patients. The technology is most widely used for monitoring heart conditions and diabetes. Medical wearable devices will be joined by activity detection sensors, sleep environment monitoring systems, indoor air quality monitoring sensors, among others to enhance patient monitoring for more effective home-based self-care. Virtual reality (VR) equipment and headsets will double up as devices capable of providing engaging physiotherapy that encourages self-regulated rehabilitation. As wearable technologies become more widely available and cost-effective, wearables are finding a role in many elements of care delivery. Remote patient monitoring enabled by wearables is expected to be a gamechanger for the healthcare industry struggling with ballooning costs. Owing to technological advancements such as IoT and software development, there has been a shift in the design of wearables focused on wellness to tools that allow physicians to remotely track and monitor patients’ vital signs, ranging from blood pressure to oxygen saturation, in real-time. Wearables have the potential to improve patient outcomes by equipping providers with real-time data collection, increasing accuracy and informed decision making. Wearable devices can capture measures of physical activity, promoting continuous monitoring of heart rate, blood pressure, glucose, and EKG. Metrics such as resting heart rate serve as an important metric to gauge cardiovascular health, enabling physicians to identify early signs of illness or health issues. Integration of health and fitness apps with wearable technology can be helpful for patients suffering from chronic diseases. It can monitor minute-to-minute chronic conditions of the patients. For example, patients suffering from diabetes are prone to the risk of large fluctuations in blood sugar level. With wearable technology, it is possible to track these fluctuations which help insulin treatment to be tailored with high precision.
  • Telemedicine Goes Mainstream - The pandemic has made telemedicine popular pushing the concept to more mainstream acceptance. Telemedicine is the very definition of digitally enabled healthcare and works as a lifesaver for the millions residing in rural parts without access to good medical care. The reach of telemedicine is seen as a major plus, where populations in remote areas can be treated in real time. This technology once it takes root as a standard is going be a game changer for the poor who cannot access and afford current health care. A new trend indicates telemedicine is gaining popularity in urban areas as well with more and more patients opting for remote diagnosis and treatment as a convenient option. The introduction of telemedicine is one of the remarkable developments in the healthcare domain. Telemedicine refers to the practice of improving patient’s health remotely through the exchange of medical information through electronic communication technologies. Telemedicine is a technology enabled delivery of medical service where the doctor and the patient do not have to be in the same place physically at the same time for consultation or diagnosis. With this new technology, patients can show themselves to a doctor for visual inspection as well as share reports on a mobile or computer screen. Technology driven cost savings, convenience, and features have helped telemedicine make a mark in the healthcare space in a short time. The advent of advanced IT infrastructure is also driving increasing use of satellite- and mobile-based communications among telemedicine providers. The switch to remote telemedicine has also been heavily facilitated by cloud technology. With the increasing prevalence of new digital health tools and services, companies are actively seeking innovative new solutions such as telemedicine to boost their employee’s health and wellness. By enabling employers to engage in preventative healthcare approaches, telemedicine initiatives help employers to reduce healthcare costs, improve healthcare access, and increase employee productivity and satisfaction. Services such as direct access to healthcare professionals via video and voice technologies, remote patient monitoring, and wellness counselling offer increased access to healthcare and greater convenience for employees at an affordable price point than traditional doctor visits.
  • Digital Technologies to Finally Destigmatize Mental Healthcare - Digital therapeutics holds critical significance given its ability to help the healthcare system in addressing the considerable gap between the need and availability of mental healthcare services. The pressing scenario created the requirement of mental health apps and other digital therapeutics powered by emerging technologies such as AI-based chatbots and big data systems for remote consultations. These solutions played an important role in pushing the availability of digital health services to people affected by mental health disorders. Various agencies leveraged cloud-based solutions for prediction of mental disorders like stress, anxiety and depression using data from specific locations like social media platforms. On the other side, the domain witnessed high uptake of AI chatbots that use pre-programmed content along with decision-trees to support automated conversations by using techniques like natural language processing. The interactive nature of these digital tools resulted in enhanced patient engagement. AI chatbots dedicated to support mental health cover solutions that facilitate counseling for improving the psychological wellbeing of people. Advanced AI chatbots for digital mental wellbeing are capable of supporting people suffering from depression and delivering cognitive behavioral therapy to individuals with depression or anxiety symptoms. These digital tools are anticipated to be used beyond the COVID-19 pandemic and favor mental health-related efforts. In addition, the domain is anticipated to receive a major boost from broader acceptance of telemedicine platforms that rely on specific technologies like videoconferencing, mobile health apps and remote telemonitoring using connected devices.
  • Integrated Diagnostics Makes Significant Headway - Integrated Diagnostics is a fast growing area, wherein enterprises have connected, smart systems that offer actionable and in-depth insights regarding their patient populations by combining monitoring, longitudinal, genomics, laboratory, and imaging data. Case in point is the multidisciplinary tumor boards. Care teams, with the use of integrated diagnostic solutions, can provide patient-oriented cancer care, integrating patient data from various sources into dashboards, thereby allowing collaborative treatment, diagnostics, and other associated aspects. The aim of this healthcare technology is not the replacement of humans but to provide support to care teams with appropriate guidance based on evidence to facilitate better decision making involving patients for enabling them to have a better life. This is also being witnessed in cardiology, wherein integrated diagnostics improved by predictive analytics and smart algorithms are enabling quicker decision making by care teams by offering suitable, actionable data that aids in improving patient care.
