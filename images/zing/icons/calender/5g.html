WHAT'S TRENDING

5G or the 5th generation of mobile connection brings with it the promise of increased bandwidth, enhanced coverage and faster speeds. The technology is poised to emerge as the mobile communications technology protocol ushering speed, connectivity, and possibilities. The 5G technology offers high bandwidth and network speed for supporting high-quality video conferencing, even across remote locations.


5G network and telecom industry in general has progressed considerably. Unlike their earlier purely connectivity role, telecom networks have evolved to create a robust ecosystem comprising of various stakeholders mainly due to the fact that wireless is a highly complex space. 5G is deemed to be the future of the mobile industry, bringing forth leading edge technologies and innovations that provide more effective communication. The 5G helps bridge the hardware design handicaps as further sophistication in software for streaming videos at extremely high speed will be required for faster speeds of Internet, and for sending input to the servers at the same speed. Another big benefit associated with 5G will be a decrease in latency that enables faster and high speed data transmission. Though the switch from 4G to 5G network has begun, it may take some time for the transition to complete. 5G network technologies that will be deployed between 2020 and 2030 will offer features such as stable connections, zero-distance connectivity for machines connected to people, and uploads and downloads at extremely fast speeds, stated to be 10-100 times the speed gains over 4G networks. 5G is expected to make more premium game titles accessible to wider audience through mobile 4G network also make it difficult to for real-time multiplayer on mobile networks, especially on a competitive level. 5G offers the ability to access simultaneous connections and bigger multiplayer experience. The network is expected to bring in more subscription services.


5G can make significant inroads toward making human lives smarter, safer, and connected. Instant interaction and communication and seamless access to information can enhance the quality of life. SBA (services-based architecture), software-defined network, and AI/ML along with technologies like Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality can support smart manufacturing, smart cities, smart utilities, machine communications, and telemedicine. Smart devices, sensors, wearables, and smartphones can realize their true potential. 5G can transform a movement away from just data and voice services to advanced services such as robotics, AR/VR, V2X, gaming, IIoT, and IoMT. AI-native services will evolve as the platform moves towards 6G technology. 5G involves not only high-speed connectivity and capacity but will augment reliability, coverage, and latency.

With significantly faster mobile networks, consumers and businesses can experience reduced energy usage, high capacity, low latency, massive connectivity, and enhanced reliability. 5G Monetization Applications


Some of the promising applications of 5G:

  • Autonomous vehicle control is where there is a need for high reliability and low latency to facilitate vehicle-to-people, vehicle-to-vehicle, and vehicle-sensor communications.
  • Standalone video applications
  • Smart factories and automation wherein the controllers must communicate with the actuators in real-time for optimum working and reducing the reliance on human intervention
  • Handling stadium and outdoor events where there is high connectivity density in a small geographic area
  • IoT devices that are located over a geographic area but connected through networks to transmit small data volumes with low density using sensors and actuators
  • Remote medical examinations and surgeries necessitate highly reliable communications and low latency
  • Smart cities with smart utility meters have a high density of low-power devices
  • Traffic in smart cities can be better managed with sensors. Emergency services can also be efficiently coordinated to provide first responders and emergency teams with optimal routes
  • Wearables that need moderate latency at lower densities and data transmission rates
  • A smart power distribution grid requires 5G to handle high volumes of telemetry data and ensure precise synchronization, detection of changes, and taking steps accordingly
  • Games, Virtual reality, and Augmented reality need very high bandwidth and low latency to provide the real-time feel
  • Precise device location and satellite technologies are facilitated by 5G

Rise in Preference for Standalone (SA) 5G Deployment Model: Most 5G networks were earlier deployed as non-standalone (NSA) networks, taking advantage of existing investments and LTE architecture. Operators capitalize on the existing LTE architecture and existing equipment. There is a need for RAN (radio access network) to be implemented but the core network can use the existing EPC (evolved packet core) from LTE. The need for additional CAPEX is lower. In NSA models, telcos can get the network ready sans additional time and money. They can unlock new 5G revenue streams. In case of Standalone (SA) 5G models , the RAN and the service-based architecture (SBA) are new and must be compliant with 3GPP recommendations. There is a clear demarcation between different network functions. This results in end-to-end high speed and service. New services can be quickly introduced with rapid time-to-market.


  • Private Network Ecosystem to Drive Demand: DSPs (digital service providers) and CSPs (communications service providers) are evolving to include the rapid growth of 5G business opportunities and the IoT. This includes private networks for some industries such as transport, utilities, Industry 4.0, and smart cities. Private networks are given certain frequencies. In the coming years, the telco equipment and services segment will grow to tens of billions of dollars. Private 5G networks will connect several products, equipment, people, tools, and heavy machinery. Each organization and industry will have its specific requirements but all of them will require high levels of security and reliability.
  • 5G Network Slicing Technology: Network slicing technology can be offered as a service to different vertical industries. This facilitates the emergence of network tenants who own a slice of the network. 5G network slicing can facilitate the creation of new partners and business enablers. The network slices will have specific QoS (quality of service) requirements. It is essential to provide isolation as several tenants will be competing for network resources.
  • Network slicing technology enables 5G digitization of several applications. Slices are created on-demand with URLLC (ultra-reliable low-latency) services. Network applications and functions are collated at the edge thereby reducing issues of multi-hop communications. Different slices might have stringent URLLC requirements and might need to operate simultaneously. They will be served by physical resources that are delivered by edge data centers and different radio sites. If two complicated surgeries are taking place simultaneously, they must both not be impacted by other services operating on the same network.
