WHAT'S TRENDING

Since the launch of Sputnik in 1957, space tech has undergone considerable advancement. Several satellite data applications have been created such as devices for weather forecasting, remote sensing, satellite navigation, and satellite TV, among others. Space tech or space technology relates to the use of engineering concepts for the development and operation of systems and devices that are used for space exploration and travel. Space technology is employed for understanding the universe, exploration of planets, and to offer navigation and communication services. It comprises various systems such as satellites and others and also the various elements of space travel. It also includes R&D of novel technologies, new uses of current technologies, and advancement of technologies for the present space systems. Robotics, ML, and AI technologies are gaining increasing application in this area, with growing launch of satellites and other systems into orbit. Space industry is increasingly leveraging emerging technologies like 5G, 3D printing, big data, quantum computing and advanced satellite systems to upgrade space operations. Several services like GPS navigation, long distance communication, satellite television, remote sensing and weather forecasting increasingly rely on space infrastructure. The emerging spacetech trends like space robotics, space traffic management, and smart propulsion are also presenting growth opportunities

Across the world, regulatory bodies and agencies have been increasingly focusing on the aspect of space sustainability. In 2022, a reduction was made by the FCC in the time limit for de-orbiting of satellites after the completion of their mission to 5 years from the previous 25 years. More restrictions are likely to be implemented via the introduction of licensing. With the prevailing supply chain issues on account of the pandemic and the war, in-house construction of systems or company acquisitions are very likely. Also, companies are expected to restrict their sourcing to their domestic boundaries. There is also likely to be greater emphasis on earth-associated applications. While deep space missions are expected to gain momentum, focus is anticipated to increase on technologies that will aid in creating a sustainable earth. Carbon use, forestry, and climate change are the areas that are likely to gain priority. Datasets including SAR, thermal, and hyperspectral are likely to witness greater growth as compared to RGB/ multispectral imaging. Big-size rockets are expected to gain growing use.

Key Space Tech Application Areas

  • Robotics - Like in various other industries, robots are making their way into the space industry, especially for exploration purposes. Robots are being sent into space for various tasks such as research, search and rescue, construction, and maintenance.
  • Space Tourism- This relates to the travel of people to space for a certain period of time. SpaceX and Virgin Galactic are some of the companies that are making efforts to offer space tourism.
  • Astrophysics and Astronomy - The application of space technology in these areas has offered important insights and information regarding the universe. The Chandra X-Ray Observatory and the Hubble Space Telescope are the main space telescopes that have been critical to astronomy and astrophysics.
  • Earth Science - This pertains to the analysis of Earth’s physical characteristics. Space tech has enabled scientists to detect oil deposits, mineral resources, and identify environmental changes. The application of space tech in earth science has provided increased awareness and critical information about Earth.
  • Remote Sensing - This refers to information collection about the elements present on the Earth from space. It aids in calculating aspects such as pollution levels, crop output, water levels, wind speed, and temperature, among others. Such data is employed for monitoring weather, the environment, and to understand changes in the climate.
  • Space Medicine - This relates to the medical issues that can emerge during space exploration. There exist various risks such as microgravity, decompression sickness, and cosmic radiation with space travel and space medicine enables in lowering these risks. Space medicine had also enabled in performing surgical procedures in space.
  • Space Architecture - This refers the construction of space structures such as space habitats, the ISS (International Space Station), and spacecrafts, launch pads, and rockets, among others.
  • Space Engineering - This is the field of design and production of space systems such as the Hubble Space Telescope, among others and also their operation and maintenance.
  • Space Mining - This is the sourcing of metals, minerals, and such others from space. Space technology has facilitated space mining for the detection and extraction of various resources.
  • Satellite Communication - Satellites facilitate communication across the world. They transmit TV signals, help manage nuclear weapons, track weather changes, among others. Satellites leverage radio waves and enable wireless data transmission. Further, they facilitate the use of mobile phones, GPS, and other kinds of communication.
  • Astrobiology - This refers to the research about any existence of life in other planets in the universe. Also, it offers an understanding of the beginnings of life on planet Earth. It enables in studying the association between planets and their moons and any existence of life on other planets.

Select Innovative Space Tech Companies

  • RocketLab: This company based in New Zealand provides satellite launches and microlaunch services for commercial and government customers. The company leverages expendable and small rockets known as Electron vehicles for satellite launch.
  • SpaceX: This private enterprise incorporated in the year 2002 creates sophisticated launch vehicles for use in civil, military, and commercial applications. In 2006, the launch of its Falcon 1 rocket led the company to be the pioneering private company to introduce a spacecraft into orbit. The company also launched the Dragon spacecraft, which had a test flight in 2010, and in 2013 a commercial cargo flight. In 2016, the company launched the Falcon Heavy rocket, the most powerful rocket in the world.
  • SpaceX: Is known for lowering the launch cost and commencing the NewSpace concept. Having about 3500 satellites in the Starlink constellation, the company is vying to become a provider of space services that is fully integrated. In 2022, the company started Starshield, offering EO functions for government agencies in the US.
  • Virgin Galactic: This is a company engaged in space tourism and offers suborbital flights (commercial) for tourists. The first human space flight (commercial) and SpaceShipTwo’s first test flight are the main achievements of the company.

  • The space industry while is robust, it however also faces various regulatory and technical challenges. The technologies employed in space missions are complex, they have a high cost and their maintenance is complicated. As space exploration entails considerable risks and is expensive, obtaining funding is generally fraught with various hurdles. High level technical knowledge is required for developing space technologies, but the availability of such workforce is limited.
  • The space industry also has a high rate of failure than other industries. Satellites that are employed to offer communication services have considerable complexity and are prone to breakdowns and issues owing to various reasons.
  • The industry is also highly regulated, with governments controlling the launch, use and access to space technologies and outer space and various other aspects such as flight safety, space resources, and space waste.
  • Poor initiative with regards to space exploration is also an issue the industry faces, mainly due to low awareness of the advantages of space exploration.
  • Further, space exploration has a negative environmental impact due to the pollution caused during satellite launch, and space debris that result from space activities.

  • AI: The robots and rovers that are used in space are already AI-enabled to a certain extent and the technology is being increasingly used for data analysis and decision making. AI might be employed for enhancing the route of rockets or for the review of the data obtained from experiments conducted on the satellites with the use of AI on Edge. While the use of AI in spacetech has mainly been the application of ML for the review of the huge quantum of data that satellites provide, the use of the technology in missions is not yet significant. In 2022, Palantir and Satellogic initiated the construction of an Edge-AI-enabled satellite in collaboration with SpaceX for the Transporter 4 mission. The NewSat of Satellogic will use the Edge AI technology of Palantir for processing of image data on orbit, lowering latency for quicker inference, and optimization of bandwidth.
  • Entry of Private Companies: With the cost of satellite and rocket launches on a downward trend, more number of private companies are expected to enter into the space sector. Virgin Galactic, Blue Origin, and SpaceX are some of the companies that have already forayed into the market and are making waves with regards to space tourism and creation of reusable rockets.
  • Cleaning of Orbital Debris: It is estimated that prior space missions have led to nearly 8000 tons of debris and there also exist various non-functional satellites present in the Earth’s orbit. These can pose risk to upcoming space missions through collisions and also can hamper the present space services such as GPS and weather forecasts. Space sustainability is therefore becoming a key focus area. NASA has created the ADRV (Active Debris Removal Vehicle) for space cleaning and is also providing funds to such projects. Similarly, ISRO is introducing and supporting projects that are focused on debris removal from the LEO (low earth orbit). The End of Life Services by Astroscale-Demonstration (ELSA-d) program introduced in 2021 also concentrates on debris elimination with the use of magnets. RemoveDebris is a waste disposal spacecraft which will employ nets to acquire floating debris.
  • Observatories: In 2021, the James Webb Space Telescope was launched. The Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope is a wide field near-infrared telescope but does not have UV coverage. NASA in 2023 is aiming to launch a space observatory TOLIMAN in the LEO. The CNSA in China is launching Xuntian in the LEO whose field of view is 300 times greater as compared to the Hubble Space Telescope. Many more observatories are expected to be launched in the forthcoming years which will critical for various space missions and activities.
  • Solar Energy: Space technology is now being leveraged for attaining the environment related goals on Earth. Solar power units that are space-based are being developed for possibly providing clean energy on earth. Capturing solar energy in space is considered to become a possibility sometime in the future. In 2023, Caltech Space Solar Project introduced such a mission. In the US, the AFRL (Air Force Research Laboratory) is focusing on important projects that are required for creating such systems in the Space Solar Power Incremental Demonstrations and Research project.
  • Space 2.0: With on-orbit processing feasible, the functionalities of space-based systems is expected to translate into commercial applications. Real unlimited on-orbit reconfigurability offers the flexibility with regards to hardware and software that these systems require to deploy and speed-up real-time AI functionalities. The use of organic packaging will facilitate the I/O and processing needed of new-age systems. With more ease in design, analysis, and reuse of space-based IP, OEMs are likely to witness the advantages of design agility.
  • With AI being aboard, systems need to be capable to support greater throughput and processing. An adaptive architecture is needed for on-orbit processing in order that systems can analyze, process, and readjust themselves for enhancing responsiveness and performance. This is resulting in the development of organic packaging. For constructing these sophisticated systems, more agility with regards to design is needed to quicken development, have lesser costs, and speed-up launch time. With more number of private enterprises entering the arena, Space 2.0 is emerging.
  • On-orbit reconfigurability and real innovation are expected to be facilitated by soft-core processors underpinned by solutions for ML-oriented applications, intelligent 3D analytical placing solutions, sophisticated routing to remove congestion, domain-oriented architectures and AI-engines, chip-to-chip interconnect and chiplet technology, and 2.5/3D die integration and packaging technology, among others
  • Growing Investments: The space industry is expected to witness healthy growth, supported by rising defense budgets due to increasing geopolitical issues and the Russia-Ukraine war. There is also growing focus on ensuring satellites and datasets produced in orbit are resilient to attacks.
  • Mobile Phone Connectivity- Direct-to-mobile connectivity from space is garnering the attention of many in the industry such as Apple, SpaceX, and Globalstar, among others. Apple and Globalstar have joined forces for offering SOS connectivity with the novel iPhone 14. T-Mobile is aiming to start LEO connectivity through SpaceX in 2023. Amazon is expected to launch LEO satellites for the Kuiper project. Many of these projects will only offer low-bandwidth connectivity for emergency texts and calls.
  • Space Agencies - In the last several years, several space agencies have been established in many countries such as New Zealand, Portugal, UAE, and Costa Rica, among others. In 2021, 4 novel space agencies came into being, and in 2023 regions such as Singapore, Yemen, Oman, Iceland, among others have announced intentions to create a space agency. With this, there are likely to be more advancements.
  • Reusable Rockets - Launch systems that are reusable are expected to considerably drive down the cost of launching into space, which are likely to facilitate the completion of many space initiatives. Further, it is expected to lower the cost of standard space missions and the process of restocking the International Space Station. In 2022, SN20 of SpaceX is expected to try the launch of the orbital flight with the use of a reusable rocket. Blue Origin is focusing on making an effort to launch its reusable 2-stage New Glenn rocket into LEO. The rocket can be used about 25 times.
  • Low Cost, Lightweight Satellites - The declining cost of launching satellites and the increasing number of applications for the data they offer are fueling the number of satellite launches. Lightweight and smaller satellites are emerging, which is allowing even startup companies to enter the space. 3D printed satellites are also being developed. Fleet Space Technologies is expected to launch such satellites which will offer connectivity and communications solutions for IoT devices.
  • LEO Constellations: With this, a huge number of applications can be facilitated. Constellation of small-sized craft can offer real-time imaging which can be leveraged for combating fires on the ground, for tracking and detection of planes with the use synthetic aperture radar and hyperspectral cameras, and for navigation purposes, among others. Further, LEO satellites translate to short-term missions which lowers costs and risk. The use of satellites in this manner can augment space innovation, facilitating transition to latest packaging technologies and process nodes sooner. With increase in on-orbit processing that needs greater I/O slots and compute, BGA (ball-grid array) packaging is gaining momentum. There is also a significant rise in development agility being witnessed, leading to quicker analysis and introduction of novel technology.
  • Organic Packaging: For space-grade systems, a transition is being witnessed in the industry towards flip-chip packaging and organic packaging. Besides supporting the I/O and the bigger die size required, the dependability of organic packaging is considered to be advantageous. The transition to this will however not be immediate.
  • Machine Learning: For overcoming design challenges, processing is expected to be done on-board instead of the ground station. Satellites are expected to process data and transmit information about the meaning of the data. For this, satellites will need to have AI capabilities in orbit, such as image categorization and object identification, among others.
  • AI is a constantly changing area and ML models need continued optimization. An adaptive and flexible architecture is required for space-based systems which can alter algorithms and models promptly. As ML requires greater compute, hardware acceleration needs to offer responsiveness in real-time. Upon the changing of algorithms, the hardware that is required for algorithm acceleration also undergoes change. Therefore, hardware and software that can be configured is required for an adaptive platform which can update along with one another. In other words, for on-orbit processing, systems should be capable of processing, analyzing, and reconfiguring up until the application code.
