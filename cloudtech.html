WHAT'S TRENDING

Digital transformation is poised to leap forward with cloud technologies with potential to take the trend from mere uptake of digital technologies to a major overhaul of tools, experiences and processes in a virtual environment with anytime, anywhere accessibility. The cloud is a critical ingredient of the digital transformation journey as it allows organizations to lift the customer experience, boost employee productivity and expedite the evolution. Companies with obsolete IT infrastructure lack tools and processes to meet customer expectations. The rapidly changing landscape requires businesses to deliver an omnichannel experience that can be adapted in real-time. The move needs cloud-based, integrated business applications to help employees access updated insights pertaining to consumer requirements. In addition, traditional approaches and systems affect employee productivity as they lack desired flexibility and agility to adapt to new functions, to scale and to fulfill evolving demands. Legacy systems fail to ensure collaboration among team members and are vulnerable to downtime.


Multiple industries have in the past few years been transformed, in various ways, by the cloud and the global automotive industry is no exception. Cloud-based solutions for the automotive industry is rapidly increasing in number and variety that the market is currently worth a whopping US$65 billion worldwide. Cloud is critical for connected cars, shared mobility, autonomous driving, digital manufacturing, customer insights and many other aspects of the modern automotive industry that is fast evolving to serve customers for the digital era. Cloud-based solutions make operations of the automotive companies faster, better and also safer. The solutions help enhance customer experience through in-vehicle entertainment. They also reduce risk of data loss. Autonomous vehicles are the self-driving vehicles that need least manual intervention. These sense the live environment with the help of AI algorithms. ADAS leveraging sensor data and real-time HD maps are able to make riding pleasant and safer for passengers. However, the system is complex and needs continuous and rigorous testing and validation, in complex environments and on roads. Cloud-based simulators are used for the purpose as these feature higher computational abilities required for transferring huge data volumes.


Cloud based solutions also enable shared mobility which is the ride-sharing concept that is fast gaining prominence. It is a transportation mode in which vehicles are shared on the basis of needs, among users, and this helps reduce traffic issues and carbon emissions. Cloud-based applications for shared mobility can integrate a Google Map or any other map for providing live traffic updates and alternative routes with less traffic. They would also provide the estimated time that would be taken for reaching destination. Algorithms can also notify cab drivers to nearest customers which would reduce idle time for drivers and wait time for customers. Cloud technology also offers auto makers specialized tools and services for big data analytics, enabling them to gather best insights about customers. Query Engine for instance, can digest data, store it and also analyse and present it as interactive charts, graphs and dashboards. Auto makers would be able to better understand trends for improving supply chain, manufacturing, marketing and also enhance customer experience.


Cloud-based solutions also enable digital manufacturing for auto makers. Intelligent algorithms developed based on cloud data can aid manufacturers in predictive maintenance, safety, data analytics and reporting, and also real-time monitoring of various production processes. This would reduce the amount of errors in production. Costs of setting up IT infrastructure and monitoring it would also be eliminated for auto makers when they choose cloud service providers for the job. Cloud service providers enable the companies to effectively monitor their IT infrastructure and keep it up and running at all times


Cloud based solutions for the automotive industry also reduces complexity of operations to a great extent. The high computing capabilities offered by cloud service providers enable auto makers to perform complicated and large engineering simulations and tasks and also data analytics, easily. Cloud solutions provide automatic scalability for servers, on-demand data storage, and enhanced computing capabilities and thus reduce complexity. Cloud solutions also offer enhanced security for data and provide backup. Whether data from smart vehicles, manufacturing, customer sales & services, supply chain or marketing, all of it is constantly backed up. This ensures that critical data is not lost in accident wipe-outs or sudden system failures.


Another industry to witness the revolutionizing impact of cloud technology is the semiconductor and electronic design automation (EDA) industry. One of the most common cloud services used in the semiconductor industry is Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS), through which EDA companies deliver their customers the ability of developing and running their PDKs over the cloud. The use of PaaS for running EDA software on certain designs enable companies in avoiding the high capital investment involved in procuring servers for providing the required processing runtime and power, along with the recurrent cost associated with supporting and maintaining those resources. The high level of computing demanded by modern integrated circuit (IC) designs can prove challenging for several companies to be able to procure, install, and maintain the desired amount of computing resources required for maintaining high-speed verification runtimes and at the same ensuring that the design quality is not compromised.


Design companies can significantly benefit from cloud computing as it can offer them an opportunity for accelerating their time to market and speeding up innovation. These can be achieved through accelerating the process of design verification. Through avoiding the high cost involved in the acquisition and maintenance of hardware and removal of latency barriers associated with resource usage, cloud computing can allow EDA companies in gaining instant accessibility to computing resources they require, while concurrently leveraging the inherent scaling capabilities of the platform, thereby enabling them in efficiently achieving their physical verification targets, such as overnight runtimes. Moving EDA technology into the cloud can also offer a fast and cost-effective mechanism for handling late-breaking emergencies. In case all internal resources of a company are committed and a critical issue arises that requires immediate resolution, cloud computing offers the mechanism of resolving the issue without causing any major disruptions in other activities.


There are various different ways through which EDA companies can further improve the cost-effectiveness of their cloud deployments. These include using the most recent foundry-qualified rule deck for ensuring the adoption of most updated coding best practices. In addition, companies can implement hierarchical filing methodology, which allows sorting of a design into cells that can be subsequently referred to in the top design levels, thereby significantly reducing data size as well as enabling a substantial reduction in final sign-off runtimes. Operations that need multiple resources often take up all those resources even prior to the execution, resulting in most resources often remaining idle for long time periods. Through implementation of technology and processes that enable connection to resources only at the time when they are required can facilitate users in minimizing their cloud computing costs by eliminating or reducing idle resources. Moreover, selection of geographically-close cloud servers can help EDA companies in reducing their network latency time.

  • Optimizes IT Budgets - Cloud provides companies with the opportunity to save on capital & IT costs as it significantly reduces spending on software and hardware required for leveraging cloud computing. The move saves on time associated with storage, networking and computing while reducing maintenance, operational and upgrade costs. The cloud mitigates costs related to maintenance of datacenters and associated IT professionals by eliminating the expensive IT infrastructure. Organizations moving to the cloud can achieve notable cost savings as reduced infrastructure also reduces the requirement for IT workers to patch servers, update software and engage in time-intensive tasks like maintenance. The pay-as-you-go model offers agility to businesses while cost savings allow organizations to implement systems that were too expensive for them earlier. In addition, the cloud provides companies with desired elasticity to scale the environment as per their business requirements. The approach automatically scales resources that support the site for dealing with business expansion.
  • Flexibility & Scalability - The cloud provides organizations with on-demand access to applications and files anytime and anywhere. The technology allows employees to remain productive irrespective of their location while enabling businesses to deliver a consistent experience to users across all branches and offices. Being an integral element of customer service, cloud computing facilitates delivery of database, storage, networking, analytics, software and intelligence over the cloud for backups. The availability of cloud computing and related resources over the virtual media allows users to access desired information and files anywhere, which reduces commute time. Cloud computing is also marked by its scalable aspect that allows expansion of the infrastructure for handling increased load for applications.
  • Diverse Services & Better DevOps -The cloud domain encompasses a broad range of services offered by different service providers that maintain a consistent focus on addressing specific requirements of business clients. These players also ensure secure backup of resources and data to uphold the IT infrastructure. Another key aspect associated with the cloud technology is enhanced DevOps that facilitates centralized control and governance with high efficiency. The cloud architecture provides developers with better control and access to different tools to expedite the development process and time-to-market for revenue gains.
  • Enhanced Collaboration & Automatic Updates - Collaboration represents an inherent aspect of the cloud technology that allows all team members from any location to remain productive and work together. The cloud boosts collaboration among team members for flexible scheduling and fast operations. The technology allows sharing of tasks among members from different time zones. Rising significance of the cloud in the digital world can be attributed to its ability to enable automatic updates. The software-as-a-service or SaaS model makes software vendors responsible for regular updates for users. The model aids seamless service for enhanced efficiency and time savings.
  • Security & Privacy - The cloud is known for advanced services to take data security and privacy to the next level. The technology also ensures data protection even amid a disaster through backup and recovery to help organizations in maintaining business continuity. The cloud offers high security levels and minimizes the risk of data breaches for businesses relying on private or public clouds. The hybrid cloud features sophisticated and automated data security options with predictive threat analytics and high visibility. With enhanced protection against data breach and cyber-attacks, the cloud is an appealing technology for ensuring privacy and compliance.
  • Disaster Recovery - Disaster recovery-as-a-service or DRaaS represents an advanced cloud service model that allows businesses to ensure data backup over the cloud using third-party cloud computing environment. The approach encompasses disaster recovery orchestration and a SaaS solution that allows the IT infrastructure in quickly regaining the functionality. In addition to enabling faster recovery of critical systems following a disaster, the cloud provides people with remote access to systems. The cloud technology provides businesses with a secure virtual setting that mitigates concerns associated with data protection and disaster recovery.
  • Competitive Advantage - Organizations embracing digitalization are poised to exploit the cloud for better focus on key business functions and data analysis. Cloud technologies make workplaces flexible and simplify management of the complex infrastructure. Cloud computing technologies enable organizations to analyze significant volumes of data captured and transmitted by connected devices to the cloud. The approach helps organizations in gaining relevant insights into different aspects and making informed decisions for an edge over competitors. In addition to providing companies with access to data in a cost-efficient manner, cloud technologies support a number of other areas like strategic alliances, defensive strategies and cost leadership.

A dynamic and rapidly changing industry, cloud technologies are a key driver of many of the transformative technology trends shaping the IT industry. Keeping abreast of emerging trends is important to develop the right cloud strategies that will help balance expected benefits with risk exposure and increase ROI on project spending.


  • Content Management Will Move to the Cloud - Cloud-based enterprise content management (ECM) is poised to gather considerable momentum over the coming years as a result of the growing need to govern and manage burgeoning content volumes. While the content generated globally is doubling every three months, around 80% of the content intended to be leveraged by knowledge workers for revenue-generation business goals remains unstructured. The staggering growth of the unstructured data is providing a major impetus to the cloud-based ECM. Cloud-based ECM enables companies to efficiently manage the content without the need to invest in associated infrastructure. The solution also enables efficient document management and makes documents centralized for quick search. The software links all documents to appropriate accounts to ensure seamless search. The software enables authorized people to access the content and data from anywhere using different devices. The centralized location for electronic document storage reduces the requirement of physical cabinets in offices. ECM protects and secures documents to help businesses in controlling access to the information on the basis of user profiles.
  • Cloud Orchestration - Automation and cloud orchestration are technologies that are intended to automate repetitive processes. However, the two are not the same. Automation is a subgroup of cloud orchestration, implying that orchestration coordinates various automated activities. Automation can be referred to as enabling repetition of a task without operator intervention or with minimum involvement. Considering that the cloud platform involves numerous moving parts, orchestration is designed to minimize staff endeavors by combining and streamlining the various tasks as a single process. The tasks include scaling, dependency management and failure recovery in addition to various other tasks. A single process can be effectively automated using automation tools while cloud orchestration facilitates the flow of automated processes. A workflow can be created encompassing numerous automated processes beginning with the initial request all the way to completion using cloud orchestration tools. The workflow basically automates the entire process of service delivery. Complex processes, involving various resources, across numerous cloud domains can be coordinated using cloud orchestration tools, which ensure overall process control in order to meet client needs. Cloud Orchestration also prevents security breaches d. Compliance testing, configuration validation and regular vulnerability scans are simplified though the use of cloud orchestration. Errors that stem during configuration set-up can be avoided owing to the implementation of standardized configuration and the ability to re-use the same for other processes.
  • Cloud Security - Integration of this critical IAM infrastructure of organizations, onto the cloud architecture, is gaining traction. Cloud IAM solutions provide detailed access control as well visibility, which enable organizations to centrally manage their cloud resources and provide secure access to the resources, for users. Access control can be more efficiently managed with cloud IAM as it defines who has what kind of access for specific resources. Cloud-hosted IAM can be private, public or a combination of both. For companies from varied end-user industries, cloud IAM offers critical benefits including synchronization as well as provisioning of identities, federation service, single sign-on features and customer identity management among others. Enterprises are able to reduce the complexity and costs involved in managing compliance and access control, by adopting cloud for IAM. Another security technology gaining prominence is cloud security gateways/broker include a combination of multiple types of security solutions such as, single sign on, encryption, tokenization, logging, API control, key management, fine-grained access controls, advanced security intelligence data for Security Information and Event Management (SIEM), among others. Cloud security gateways (CSGs), also called as CASBs, act as a gatekeeper for organizations accessing cloud resources, allowing them to extend the reach of security policies beyond their on-premise infrastructure.
  • The Future is Hybrid – Hybrid clouds bridge the gap between internal private and external public clouds and enable an organization to get maximum value from their IT through priority based dispersal of workload. For instance, hybrid clouds help organizations leverage the power of private clouds for managing mission critical workloads such as BI analytics, financial analysis and forecasting, while transferring non-critical workloads such as HR & IT processes to the public cloud. While external facing applications are usually used on a public cloud, internal applications are used on a private cloud. By helping them limit use of expensive but secure private clouds only for critical applications and leverage inexpensive public cloud for non-critical applications, the hybrid cloud model allows organizations to reduce their total cost of their cloud deployments and at the same time be assured of security and scalability to their key applications and data. Hybrid cloud is also a cost-effective solution for managing temporary increases in computing needs as it provides the much required scalability and flexibility in handling them without the need for deploying additional physical infrastructure. Given the benefits of hybrid cloud in balancing priority based workloads and reducing the need for expensive private cloud infrastructure, hybrid cloud deployment model is now emerging as the future of cloud computing and a necessity for companies to operate in the 21st century.
  • Cloud Exchanges Emerge as the New generation of Cloud Brokers – Cloud exchanges are presently in their early stages of evolution. This business model set into motion by the cloud commoditization trend will interestingly push the trend further in the coming years. Trading cloud services on a commodities future exchange market is the new reality that will revolutionize the consumption of cloud services. Neutral cloud exchanges will help companies buy IT cloud resources for the future based on the current best price, allowing companies to hedge capacity needs and control costs by buying futures on prices. In other words, future compute capacity can be bought in bulk at current prices. As cloud begins to be traded as a commodity, there is increasing potential for speculation. Spot prices of cloud resources will fluctuate significantly depending on current supply and demand conditions. The concept of an open marketplace for trading cloud services both for spot and futures, has several advantages and benefits. One of the major factors which have pushed cloud exchange into a commercially viable concept is the development of tools to quantify cloud workloads and measure IT infrastructure usage. The successful development of vendor neutral cloud exchanges can emerge to a challenge to cloud brokerage services, in the long-term period.
  • Edge Computing, an Emerging Cloud Trend - Edge computing is rapidly emerging as a networking technology innovation that can be widely adopted across industries. Edge computing involves taking a part of the cloud computing power and bringing it closer to devices located near the edge of a network. Edge meets local computing requirements as data is processed in micro-data centers. On-site data processing helps to eliminate latency and address network and connectivity challenges. IoT devices form the backbone of edge computing as they enable data analysis and processing on the edge. Every edge computing unit would have dedicated storage, computer and network system. Load balancing, networking, routing, switching, as well as security would be handled by the devices. The devices together form a network which processes data from various sources. Event processing engines would analyze the data points to determine data streaming route. Edge computing enables adoption of real time advanced capabilities including analytics and AI. Data processing close to source enables quick response time. Edge facilitates creation of a robust platform that allows communities, partners, and others to access a company’s data, applications, systems and services. The access is facilitated though open data, cloud services, APIs, and self-service portals among other means. Platform network participants would be able to access insights and compute power to add value to service and product portfolio.

Since data collection for algorithm training involves significant level of storage space and computing power, organizations consider as-a-service model as a prudent move for cost savings. Cloud-based datacenters offer desired bandwidth and computation power for generating training data for AI or machine learning solutions. Providers of cloud services are also relying on artificial intelligence for various tasks such as management of distributed vast networks to provide customers with storage resources, support to cyber-security solutions and management of cooling or power systems in datacenters. Leading players are exploiting AI technology for development of cost-efficient cloud services. The combination of cloud computing and artificial intelligence is paving way for insight-driven, strategic and more efficient organizations. While cloud computing offers agility, flexibility and cost-saving avenues, artificial intelligence enables efficient management of data and related analytics, insights, better workflows and enhanced customer experience. AI-as-a-service is anticipated to improve and transform cloud computing platforms and services while offering notable boost to innovation. The combination reduces infrastructure management costs to help organizations in becoming more flexible and agile. A large number of organizations are using the multi-cloud option for accessing specialist services from different cloud providers. Businesses are moving from single cloud for management of different needs towards multi-cloud platforms for mixing and matching services as per their requirements. The multi-cloud strategy frees organizations from sticking to a single ecosystem and improves redundancy to reduce system downtime or errors from critical failures. Multi-cloud infrastructure is witnessing increasing popularity among organizations looking for a flexible and hybrid cloud environment that can enable security and cost savings gains. The multi-cloud architecture helps businesses in enhancing ROI, boosting security & disaster protection, reducing latency, and driving autonomy. Companies can mix and match various cloud environments for leveraging best solutions for specific requirements, leading to enhanced return on investment. The multi-cloud architecture enables the comprehensive cloud setting that offers an appealing blend of cost savings and data security. The multi-cloud architecture reduces the requirement for data to move across different nodes to lower latency and expedite performance. In addition, the multi-cloud strategy provides organizations with more freedom and autonomy to distribute their workloads across various environments. The infrastructure allows users to enhance and update the cloud environment while ensuring enhanced security and performance. The architecture also prevents organizations from placing all cloud resources in a single ecosystem to ensure access to desired tools in case of issues with a specific environment.
  • Serverless Computing Hits the Spotlight - Also referred to as functions-as-a-service, serverless computing has emerged as a fastest-growing PaaS cloud services, and is poised to witness immense popularity over the coming years. The service eliminates the requirement for organizations to invest in large-sized servers and provides users with the flexibility to expand server requirements as per their needs. Providers of serverless computing charge clients on the basis of cloud usage rather than a fixed bandwidth or server count. While the service involves the use of servers, organizations are not involved in technical aspects and configurations. The cloud model makes it convenient for users to access backend services without focusing on infrastructure management. The option allows organizations to pay more attention to products and projects rather than focusing on backend. Serverless computing allows developers to develop and deploy codes without worrying about provisioning of cloud resources. As automation is responsible for provisioning and configurations for servers, the model helps developers in concentrating on coding. The service model plays an important role in fostering innovation and enabling organizations to deliver exciting experiences to customers.

  • Cloud Gaming to Hit Primetime - Following the impressive success of cloud-enabled streaming services such as Netflix that have transformed the way people consumer music, TV and movies, the cloud is set to take streaming video gaming forward. Cloud gaming allows users to play video games that are hosted on remote servers. Users stream video games on their devices via the Internet.   • Secure Access Service Edge Gains in Prominence - Secure access service edge (SASE) is gaining increasing significance due to broader adoption of the remote working options in the wake of pandemic. SASE represents a network architecture that combines cloud-native network security components and software-defined WAN to ensure seamless remote access. Some of the key assets related to the approach are secure web gateways, zero-trust network access, cloud access security brokers and firewalls-as-a-service. As a cyber-security aspect, SASE creates a highly secure link between services or applications and various components of an organization, including devices, systems and people. Increasing digitalization and ongoing transfer of security architecture into the cloud is driving the significance of cloud to provide a WAN capacity for supporting digital businesses. In addition, SASE is anticipated to find broader acceptance due to its ability to deliver secure web gateways and protect businesses from cyber-threats.
  • Robust Adoption on Cards for No-Code & Low-Code Cloud Services - Organizations are looking for advanced platforms and tools to develop new applications and exploit data for addressing different issues without even engaging in creating computer codes. The need is paving for increasing attention on no-code and low-code solutions that encompass programs to create web applications, websites and various digital products. No-code and low-code solutions are emerging as a major cloud computing trend, and finding acceptance to create AI-based applications. A major fraction of these services and tools are offered through the cloud to help organizations in accessing them as-a-service, which eliminates the requirement for companies to spend on the computing infrastructure. Latest tools available on the market are enabling companies to perform tasks such as development of web applications, website designing and automation of spreadsheets without gaining expertise in coding. These services are slated to enjoy increasing popularity and receive a significant level of support from the cloud technology.
  • Blockchain & Cloud Convergence - The implementation of blockchain and associated parts is anticipated to be a big trend in the field of cloud computing. While blockchain faces various scalability concerns, especially related to storage and management of large data volumes, it is an appealing technology with intriguing merits for businesses. Based on their unique features, blockchain systems are capable of boosting performance, functionality and security. Cloud computing and IoT environments often rely on centralized servers for various tasks such as data management or decision making. Any problem with the central server can disrupt the entire system to cause loss of stored data. In addition, a centralized server is at high risk of cyber-attacks. The blockchain technology offers a perfect solution to the issue by allowing storage of multiple copies of same data on different computer nodes that minimize the risk of disruptions due to failure of a single server. The decentralized approach enables an architecture that allows various computers to work simultaneously for a single task such as data storage and processing to expedite operations and data processing or uploading speed. The blockchain technology for cloud computing also improves data security. Since cloud computing deals with significant data volumes, there are risks pertaining to data security due to involvement of a centralized architecture. A successful attempt to hack the centralized architecture can disrupt the entire system and leave no option for businesses to recover the lost data. With its decentralized architecture, blockchain holds significant potential to boost security levels. In addition, the cloud allows replication of blockchain data through the network of interconnected computing servers. The approach significantly reduces the risk of single failure associated with a cloud node. Moreover, blockchain and cloud computing provide organizations with a highly scalable advanced integrated system.
