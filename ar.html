WHAT'S TRENDING

Accelerated transition towards digitalization is disrupting existing business models and leading to major changes that require companies to adapt quickly with implementation of sophisticated technologies and tools. Customers in the digital world require businesses to deliver newer and engaging experiences. While the real world is marked by its three-dimensional nature, majority of the information is available on 2D screens. The gap between digital and real worlds makes it challenging for businesses to exploit the information in an efficient manner. Augmented reality relies on various technologies capable of superimposing digital images and data on real items and environments to bridge the gap. Augmented reality is poised to witness extensive adoption given its compelling benefits that allow organizations to attract and win customers, simplify processes and attain differentiation in the competitive market. The technology is anticipated to gain further from continuing technological advances with potential to dramatically transform the way people interact with other or solve problems.


Ongoing advances in the field have enabled users to enjoy immersive experiences over the web browser. In contrast to standard audio-visual experiences that exist in digital or physical world, augmented reality blends both the real and virtual worlds by placing digital atop physical components to result in a mirage effect. The technology significantly enhances the user experience to help businesses in creating a lasting impression of customers. Users can today enjoy the AR experience through mobile devices and headsets and given the extensive proliferation of these devices already emerging are new avenues for advertising and delivery of engaging experiences. Also, spatial audio or 3D audio is poised to enable engaging, immersive AR experiences. The combination of AR with VR offers a host of intriguing benefits to businesses that are likely to disrupt various industries. While mobile and retail areas are anticipated to post significant growth, the Metaverse is expected to create lucrative opportunities. In the coming years, AR is poised to become an indispensable option for businesses.


Companies using the strategy to deliver engaging AR experiences are bound to register impressive gains and stand out from competition on counts of exceptional digital experiences and enhanced user engagement. Increasing number of companies have started to move away from traditional approaches such as the print medium towards augmented reality to improve user engagement to enjoy countless gains, like higher sales and brand loyalty. Businesses are using numerous AR options such as store signage, scan-friendly product labels, catalogs and interactive ads for elevating user engagement. The ability of AR to deliver desired content and information in a quick and efficient manner to lift the user engagement is anticipated to help the technology in garnering increased acceptance in the coming years. Retail brands are leveraging AR-powered fitting rooms that provide customers with virtual try-on opportunities at the convenience of their homes. AR supported virtual try-on feature helps cosmetic brands to enable customers conveniently try out numerous shades and colors using just their smartphones. AR is also being embraced by furniture brands to help customers in virtually viewing any product directly in their rooms without visiting the physical store. AR tools allow customers to evaluate furniture details such as depth, size and color to ensure a perfect fit in their living rooms.


AR allows companies to integrate real-time data analytics on-site. This allows users in monitoring system performance and other aspects in real-time for proactive action to push efficiency and productivity. Augmented reality also enables visual representation of the data with relevant insights for process improvements. AR is poised to become an effective interface between machines and humans, providing businesses with a formidable technology. Leading organizations are using AR for product development, production, marketing, logistics, services and training to push productivity and quality. The technology significantly improves the way people visualize information, interact with different products, and receive or follow specific instructions.


The technology plays an important role in eliminating the cognitive overload to enable better decision making. Cognitive overload refers to a situation when the working memory faces more information for processing than it can handle. Augmented reality helps avoid cognitive overload by providing information in a clearly summarized digital snippet for enhanced understanding. This is being leveraged by various brands that are implementing AR-based mobile apps to help people in doing more without spending more time. AR allows development of immersive training programs in a cost-efficient manner and enables organizations to create immersive training modules along with effective evaluation options. AR-powered platforms can assist employees in simulating and visualizing scenarios for effective training. The technology also allows companies to develop interactive assessment options to measure training efficiency.

Several technologies are converging to enable more angles of experience and interpretation of reality. Few of technologies that provide the backbone of the AR experience are discussed below.


  • Sensors - Sensor technologies comprise an indispensable component of augmented reality systems owing to their role in providing key inputs to AR devices or platforms. Majority of AR applications focus on audiovisual interfaces and use voice recognition and motion tracking sensors. AR systems are required to understand what a user is seeing or hearing and how the surrounding is changing. This requires sophisticated sensing that relies on IMUs along with heat mapping, time-of-flight sensors and structured light sensors. IMUs for AR systems use gyroscope, magnetometer and accelerometer to correct error and deliver accurate results to help the system in tracking head position and movement. Augmented reality environments also depend on various imaging sensors for presenting digital images of a real environment. Advanced headsets for AR rely on special imaging sensors such as time-of-flight (ToF) cameras, binocular depth sensing, structured-light sensors, and vertical-cavity surface-emitting laser (VCSEL)-based light detection and ranging (LiDAR). ToF cameras are intended to combine modulated IR light source and a charged coupled device image sensor. These sensors measure time-of-flight or phase delay of reflected light for determining the distance between cameras and illuminated object. While structured light sensors are designed to project specific pattern of light onto the surrounding, VCSEL-based LiDAR produces high-fidelity point clouds. The VCSEL technology finds use in wearable devices such as smart glasses for yielding low-power, compact displays as well as gesture recognition systems. Some of the other types of sensors used by AR systems are directional microphones, ambient light sensors, rear- or forward-facing video cameras, and different biosensors like thermal sensors.
  • Location Tracking - AR depends on location tracking devices for desired outcomes such as GPS and magnetometers. Magnetometer measures the magnetic field of the Earth to provide AR devices with an orientation regarding the magnetic field. These sensors allow smartphones to find a specific direction for auto-rotation of digital maps in line with physical orientation of the user. These devices are relevant for location-based AR applications. The GPS represents a navigation satellite system intended to offer time and geo-location information to dedicated GPS receiver. These devices also play an important role in enabling location-based AR applications.
  • Haptics - Haptics for augmented reality holds a significant relevance for creating tactile effects to uplift the entire AR experience. Design engineers have developed ultrasound-based haptics on the basis of precise control of multiple speakers sending out timed ultrasound pulses. With meticulous time differences, ultrasound pulses from different speakers reach at a specific place concurrently to cause a pressure point, which can be experienced by the user skin. Users can change the focal point of ultrasound waves in real-time on the basis of specific factors like hand position. These platforms also involve the use of tracking camera for gesture recognition as well as offering exact position of the hand to change the focal point. Approaches to control movement of the focal point can result in different tactile effects. In addition, the haptic feedback is anticipated to use AV/VR platforms beyond gaming applications, such as interactions or system controls for vending machines or shopping kiosks, building automation and automotive interiors.
  • Mobile AR - The concept of mobile AR revolves around the use of mobile devices like tablets or smartphones for augmented reality. The launch of Apple ARKit in 2017 along with its integration into the iOS platform played an important role in pushing accessibility. The development was followed by Google’s launch of ARCore in the next year to support Android devices. Considering the extensive penetration of mobile devices, mobile AR has emerged as the most popular version of augmented reality. Some of the most successful uses of the concept include AR games such as Pokémon Go, Google’s AR search, face filters and apps dedicated to specific applications. While haptics is yet to find acceptance in the field of mobile AR, there are ongoing efforts that are anticipated to enable the development.
  • AR Content Creation Tools - There are several software solutions and online platforms that assist developers in creating the AR content. While options like Google’s ARCore are effective solutions for beginners, Google’s Poly presents an online library that allows users to browse, remix and share 3D assets. These assets represent 3D models or scenes generated using Blocks, Tilt Brush, or 3D programs capable of producing files that may be uploaded to Poly. Some of these assets are available for developers without any charge. Developers can use Tilt Brush for painting in 3D with virtual reality, using elements like 3D brush strokes, fire, stars or light. On the other side, developers can use Blocks to create 3D objects. In addition, cross-platform game engines such as Unity are gaining increasing attention for developing 2D and 3D simulations and video games for mobile devices, game consoles and computers. These platforms are enjoying increasing acceptance among developers for creation of AR content.
  • Motion Tracking Technologies - Augmented reality platforms rely on various motion tracking technologies for sensing the user movement. Some of the key technologies used by these platforms include Concurrent Odometry & Mapping (COM) and Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM). The former allows smartphones to locate themselves in space in line with their surroundings. The COM technology captures feature points, referred to as visually distinct objects’ features within the environment, including a table edge or light switch. On the other hand, SLAM helps smartphones and robots in understanding and analyzing the surrounding for acting in an appropriate manner. The SLAM process uses different types of sensors such as light sensors, accelerometers, cameras, depth sensors and gyroscopes.
  • AR Headsets - AR headsets hold a relatively bulky form factor to accommodate various functions associated with the augmented reality technology, including spatial tracking or 3D mapping of surrounding. Some of the known AR headsets on the market are Microsoft Hololens and Magic Leap that were first introduced in 2016 and 2018, respectively, followed by their upgraded versions in the later years. Video AR headsets are built on same principles as VR headsets. Video AR headsets feature inbuilt cameras for making video of the surrounding and transferring the data as video image to opaque screens. This results in perception of viewing the surrounding world that can be effectively augmented using digital components on displays. The display technology holds a number of merits over conventional AR headset options with transparent waveguides. In addition to offering a broader field-of-view than the view associated with VR headsets, video AR headsets enable effective masking of real objects, display of darkness or shadows, and seamless transition within the mixed reality spectrum. These merits and ongoing advances are anticipated to help video AR headsets in strengthening their position as the best hardware option for next-generation augmented reality.
  • Data Glasses - While these devices imitate traditional glasses in terms of a slim form factor, they can deliver just a basic level of AR and known for narrow field-of-view. Data glasses superimpose relevant information or data in the field-of-view of a user or beam the virtual screen into room without efficiently considering physical surroundings. Some of these devices lack a display and deliver smart functions like speakers, cameras and voice assistance. The low level of AR functionality associated with these devices can be attributed to basic level of AR miniaturization and narrow field-of-view that have limited their broader uptake.
  • AR Glasses - Technology researchers in the augmented reality industry are making serious efforts to develop comfortable and stylish AR glasses with superior AR functionality and wide field-of-view. These glasses need to project holograms into the space, effectively blend digital and analog reality, and support users in routine lives. However, there are a number of challenges in this direction that are hindering the development. A desired AR glass is required to deliver significant computing power to perform sophisticated tasks associated with AR calculations while ensuring an extended battery life. These glasses require at least two cameras for orientation and recognition of objects, places and people. In addition, a perfect pair of AR glasses is required to feature a lightweight footprint for user comfort. Ongoing developments in this direction are focusing on the use of emerging technologies such as 5G and the cloud to outsource the computing load and enable a slim form factor. On the flip side, these technologies are anticipated to result in issues regarding data security and privacy.
  • AR Contact Lenses - Taking augmented reality beyond slim, lightweight AR glasses, AR contact lenses are gaining attention from various startup companies such as Inwith and Mojo Vision that have introduced their prototypes of AR contact lenses. These prototypes are designed to display the content in a 15º field-of-view on the retina. These contact lenses rely on eye tracking to adjust the image. While AR contact lenses are an impressive hardware option in the field of augmented reality, they are likely to take some time for viable versions.

Keeping up with a dynamic industry like AR means staying on top of emerging trends in the larger business landscape. Discussed below are major trends expected to make it big in the coming year.


  • Enter the Metaverse – AR is one of the indispensable metaverse technologies intended to blur existing boundaries between real and virtual worlds. In a recent development, Meta introduced a sophisticated engine for its AR Spark Studio for creating compelling sound effects by blending multiple sounds. The innovation allows creation of multi-sensory effects to assist individuals in using sound and vision for better engagement and immersive AR experiences. AR also allows users to take things from real world into digital world or vice-versa. While currently augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) experiences continue to differ, each carving a market for itself, the future is expected to witness the convergence of these two technologies into mixed reality (MR). Offering the best of both worlds, mixed reality is expected to be the technology of the future. Benefits of mixed reality expected to drive its unassailable future include real-time view of real-world surroundings and environment and overlay of intelligent virtual objects that enables more intelligent and productive interactions; advantages of gesture- and voice-based inputs; ability to share MR experiences for a more realistic interaction; provides a more natural experience; spatial mapping capabilities; suitability with enterprise-focused applications; and continuous view of both worlds for a rich personalized experience. Mind expanding digital experience will be the way forward unlike the completely immersive digital experience offered by VR or the real world augmented with virtual elements offered by AR.
  • Integration With AI – AR and artificial intelligence presents a powerful combination that can address specific requirements of AR to enable innovative solutions. AI technology can power spatial and facial recognition software used by AR. Understanding and exploiting data from sensors require sophisticated algorithms. Artificial intelligence can significantly simplify the entire process and deliver enhanced accuracy for these applications. The technology is being used in advanced apps that assist users in digitalizing objects in the physical world into 3D objects for incorporation into specific programs. In addition, 3D scanning approach allows users to import physical items in the Metaverse. 3D scanning is anticipated to emerge as an intriguing option for businesses to expedite products for virtual trial experiences. The combination of augmented reality and AI also enables automatic design. Advanced applications in this direction allow users to draw in AR or leverage artificial intelligence for drawing items or structures in a faster manner. The potential of AI tools to design 3D objects from the real world is expected to bolster the demand for AI to create and design desired items for the physical world in the coming years.
  • Rise of WebAR - This represents a notable trend in the field of augmented reality and offers an effective option for accessibility. Supported by web browsers, WebAR eliminates the requirement for additional software for better accessibility. On the flip side, the functionality of WebAR is limited to basic augmented reality experiences. The platform also lacks various features delivered by native AR on mobile devices. Despite these limitations, WebAR is an important trend as it allows simple experiences and functions, such as addition of face filters, background replacement, simple 3D items and changing color or items or hair. In addition, WebAR can enable simple virtual try-on experiences, prompting some of the leading brands to use the platform for their cosmetic products. WebAR is likely to gain traction as an effective approach to bridge the gap between real and digital worlds. Further technological advances are anticipated to help WebAR in emerging as a compelling option for engaging with the Internet.
  • Cross-Platform AR - Cross-platform applications for AR are relatively challenging to develop and unable to match their counterpart native apps in terms of functions and experience. While cross-platform apps for AR can be easily coded and expedite the time-to-market, there are issues associated with their presentation and performance. Native applications are generally considered for apps that are complex in nature or need to exploit native features of the platform, including projects like AR navigation. In the recent years, cross-platform apps have started gaining attention for apps that are simple and need to deliver a decent performance. These apps are suitable options for tasks marked by simple product preview modules in AR. In addition, various companies are joining hands with AR development players for building high-quality cross-platform apps.
  • Next Level of Functionality for AR Glasses - While AR glasses are yet to touch the magic bullet status, they are witnessing positive progress and advances intended to enable a consumer-friendly option. In a recent development, Meta has announced a mixed-reality headset, Cambria. However, the headset is intended for the premium category to help wealthier customers in experiencing AR. On the other hand, Apple is working on LiDAR scanner in line with its attention on delivering a consumer-centric AR glasses or headset in the coming years. Future advances by the company are anticipated to eventually pave way for a comfortable and consumer-friendly AR glass. The future of augmented reality is likely to be also influenced by ongoing efforts by companies to develop innovative devices. Some of the players targeting the market are working on AR smart contact lenses that rely on software, eye tracking and communications for seamless integration with user interface for AR experience.
  • AR for Remote Assistance - The massive shift of organization towards remote working amid the pandemic has presented new avenues for the AR technology to enable remote assistance. Video conferencing applications are an integral component of remote working as it allows connectivity among employees and team members for desired discussions. AR is witnessing increasing uptake among organizations that are continuing with remote and hybrid working in the post-pandemic phase, and seek advanced tools to enable the same experience to employees as working in a real office setting. The need has led various companies to introduce advanced remote assistance solutions powered by AR. In addition, these solutions are poised to find increasing role in improving customer support and services during telework.
  • AR in Manufacturing Reaches Inflection Point - The AR technology holds significant potential for the manufacturing sector due to its relevance for applications like training and remote troubleshooting. While AR experiences with CAD data can elevate worker training programs, the technology can aid routine maintenance tasks. AR applications can provide information about devices and equipment for guiding technicians. The use of head-mounted options is a better approach in this direction in comparison to mobile apps. AR technology can also be used to provide workers with contextual information regarding various aspects within a manufacturing unit. Workers can highlight desired objects with mobile devices to learn and identify actions like maintenance. AR is anticipated to also find broader use for supporting remote troubleshooting activities. Remote support technicians can place specific virtual markers on screen to assist workers on the other side, enabling remote support.
  • AR Applications Begin to Disrupt the Automotive Industry – AR can highlight and detect on-road objects using heads-up display and scene analysis tools. This allows drivers to gain information about GPS directions and hazards without taking eyes off the road, resulting in enhanced safety. Leading companies are using the technology and AI to come up with advanced solution to assist drivers in identifying potential hazards such as nearby objects as well as redirect driver attention to the road. OEMs are also tapping the AR technology to develop advanced solutions capable of using facial recognition technology for detecting eye and head movement for drivers to issue an alarm and keep them awake. Latest cameras powered by infrared sensing and AI can track eye and head movement in darkness and detect behaviors indicating a drowsy state. Another prominent use of AR in the industry is the parking assistance. The use of 5G technology provides drivers with information about available parking spaces on their heads-up displays. The technology can be exploited for capturing significant amount of relevant data that can be used to optimize operation and design of parking spaces, including garages and parking lots. The technology is also being used by major paints brands to assist customers in virtually trying out a shade for their rooms. AR apps on mobile devices also allow mechanics in the automotive industry to make repairs. Augmented reality allows mechanics to view superimposed information and images on mobile devices for locating defective parts and repairing them in a faster manner.

Various companies are looking forward to add interactivity to specific marketing materials such as business cards to differentiate and provide customers with an exciting experience. Several companies are focusing on AR manuals to deliver feature-rich, detailed documentation and instructions to customers. These efforts are attributed to the ability of AR to deliver desired information to customers in an immersive way and take user experience to another level. The augmented reality technology also holds relevance for advertising applications, especially amid declining popularity of web banner ads that make it difficult for various customers to access the content. Ads powered by AR can ensure enhanced experience while allowing seamless access to desired content.
  • More Fluid & Viable AR Navigation - The emergence of technologies such as ultra-wideband, Wi-Fi RTT and Bluetooth Low Energy antennas is paving way for more viable and fluid indoor AR navigation. The technology can be exploited to display AR directions in various large-area indoor locations like airports, shopping malls and distribution centers, benefitting both customers and businesses. While guests can use AR indoor navigation for finding desired products in a store, employees in distribution centers can use the technology for locating a specific item within the warehouse. AR indoor navigation also holds potential to improve efficiency of ‘buy online pick-up in store’ (BOPIS) services. AR directions facilitate direct navigation for team members seeking specific items or products for order fulfillment. The approach plays an important role in saving time for employees.
  • AR to Elevate Shopping Experience - AR was one of the fortunate technologies that received a major stimulus from the pandemic for elevating the online shopping experience. The scenario resulted in extensive use of virtual try-on solutions. AR technology witnessed broader uptake among brands to take the online shopping experience to the next level. Leading brands are betting heavily on augmented reality technology and associated experiences to help customers in virtually trying products before finalizing the purchasing decision. Some of the major jewelry brands are using AR for enabling customers to personalize specific products on the website using a 3D configurator as well as virtually trying them. While traditional retail stores are witnessing return of the customer in the post-pandemic phase, there are compelling opportunities for the technology to improve the in-store experience. Various stores are using smart mirrors for enhanced experience and reducing the requirement of fitting rooms. These smart mirrors allow customers to try products in-store using sophisticated AR technology. In the coming years, AR is poised to garner more attention in the retail sector for uplifting the shopping experience for customers.
  • Education to Get More Interactive With AR - Augmented reality is being increasingly considered as an intriguing technology to overcome various limitations associated with the traditional theory-based education model. AR can enable highly interactive solutions that can boost imagination, understanding and learning. Considering the inclination of children to learn new technology and experiences, AR improves motivation and makes classes more engaging and entertaining. On the other hand, 4D Anatomy enables students to explore a large number of anatomical structures as well as discover numerous dissection specimens related to real anatomy. The approach enhances understanding of the anatomy through manipulation and observation of virtual 3D objects from multiple angles. In addition, AR presents a more affordable and accessible option over physical supplies, indicating a promising future of the technology in the field of education in the future.
