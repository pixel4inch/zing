WHAT'S TRENDING

In the past decade, UAVs have gradually transitioned from being expensive systems used solely by military agencies to an easily accessible commodity, which has opened up a slew of opportunities in the commercial space. Swift technological innovations offered consumers with Avante-Garde products at low cost and improved accessibility and associated ease-of-use, while costs have reduced substantially. As a result, drone technology is now emerging as a viable tool for individuals, government agencies, businesses and non-government organizations. Recently, the FAA loosened the regulations and rules concerning the use of small drones weighing less than 55 lbs for commercial applications. This has led to increased adoption of drones for commercial applications and helped drone technology to become mainstream. Currently, small drones can be operated at sporting events and concerts, as well as for security purposes with certain restrictions. Drones are expected to be used more extensively in e-commerce package delivery in the forthcoming years with the entry of new players and greater investments in drone delivery. The rule changes made by FAA have spurred the growth of commercial drone operations, which is expected to positively contribute to the global commercial drone industry. With continuous improvements in collision-avoidance and automated technologies, drones are anticipated to perform more complex tasks


The industry is also anticipated to witness increasing popularity of Drones-as-a-Service intended to reduce costs and risks associated with commercial use of drones. Various companies are expected to focus on drone-based artificial intelligence solutions to support machine vision and image recognition. A major development is integration of drone data with workflows for enhanced capabilities. At the same time, concerns regarding misuse of the technology along with safety, security concerns indicate the pressing need for drone countermeasure technologies. The civil UAV market is comparatively new than the military drone market. Emerging companies in both developing and developed nations, as well as early-stage start-ups are receiving funding and support from investors in the US. In India, these companies are being supported by government agencies. In addition, some universities offer UAVs-related degrees and training and research programs. Private entities also offer in-person and online training programs for both commercial and recreational UAVs use. Globally, consumer drones are being extensively used even by military organizations, because these consumer products are cost-effective.


Drone regulations are witnessing changes in most countries, especially those in the Asia-Pacific region. While evolving regulations are one driver for increased demand for UAV drones demand, the others include the thriving economies of India and China. Indeed, China is expected to dominate the UAV drones market in the region, and India is expected to register strong growth in demand for commercial drones for various applications in the country. Another major hub for drones in the Asia-Pacific region is Australia where BVLOS testing is becoming increasingly popular. The country had over 1,200 drone operators in 2019 that are focused to develop technologies and services that can support a large drone ecosystem. Further, the Civil Aviation Safety Authority has introduced drone regulations that are framed to encourage local and international players, and widen adoption of drones for myriad of applications. The Japanese drone market is likely to benefit from the recent implementation of new regulations for both manufacture and use of drones in the country. The Japanese government has been undertaking various programs to support the domestic commercial drone industry, and aims to make drone delivery a reality in Tokyo. The government seeks to set up a streamlined registry system to provide greater opportunities for commercial drones by the year 2023.


Over the years, the cost of materials used in drones has been falling steadily. This reduction in the cost of drones is being enabled by continuous decline in cost of components over the years. In addition, development of better capabilities to engineer various components into drones is also contributing to cost decline. Prominent among these is the decline in prices of carbon fiber. This is critical since cost of materials such as carbon fiber accounts for a substantial share of the total cost of drones. In addition to carbon fiber, cost of flight controllers has also been falling with around 25% decline in cost (per unit) recorded between 2015 and 2022 for flight controllers. Further, battery storage costs have also seen considerable fall, with about 16.5% decline recorded during 2015 and 2022. While the cost of energy storage is 5reducing steadily, significant improvements are seen in energy density. The cost of IR cameras used in camera-equipped drones is also reducing gradually over the years, while the accuracy of optical detectors continues to improve. Continuous decline in prices of hobbyist and commercial drones is expected to ensure that more and more civilian applications become viable. While hobbyist models are presently available for as low as $100, quad-rotor drones with HD video camera designed for commercial applications are available for about $1000.


The widespread availability of low-cost and powerful smartphone devices is enabling drones to now be controlled by smartphone-based apps. Driven by constant improvements in smartphone and tablet apps, the ability to fly, maintain and upgrade drones has become significantly easy, which is in turn offering new areas of opportunity from expanded range of end-use markets. In addition to cost reduction, advancements in smartphone technology and drone systems have led to the development of drones that are easy to fly, convenient to maintain and extremely robust. Drone flying is also becoming easy due to the use of smartphone technology, along with sensors and cameras, GPS and motion detection capabilities, and advanced software. Smartphones are now being used for controlling most of the drone functions. This shift towards smartphones opens up opportunities for the drones’ progression towards cloud, since connectivity to smartphones makes it possible for getting connected to the cloud. Drone use through smartphone apps is a market with considerable promise in the years to come. As with smartphones that transitioned from being a consumer product to finding use in industrial and medical sectors, drones are also set to a similar transition.

Drones' capability to be programmed to do a range of tasks, hold objects and fly makes drone technology revolutionary in numerous ways. Drones assist businesses, individuals, and public services, and several diverse industries are gaining an advantage from drone technology. The military uses drones for advanced weaponry and defense, social media suggests travel vloggers use drones, and the food sector is using drones for safe, easy, and faster delivery of meals.


  • Drones make Inspections Highly-Efficient - Drones assist various industries in the inspection of various situations such as plumbing, engineering, roofing, mapping, and disaster & safety management. The UAVs assist law enforcement officers in doing their work efficiently and making the specific situation more easy and safe to deal with. Crime scenes and specific areas that are risky to access such as hostage crises are easily recorded using drones. Furthermore, drones that run silently assist police with investigations while searching complicated areas to reach, and rescue hostages or identify weak spots to arrest a criminal. Drones assist repairmen and plumbers in inspecting areas situated at elevated locations without any requirement to climb high places like rooftops, which assists in determining the damage without having to climb up. The same benefit applies to places that are difficult to reach inside a building or an object that requires repair. Engineers are anticipated to benefit immensely from the usage of drones as the technology of drones enables engineers to make highly accurate calculations in the construction process. Drones are highly advantageous for engineers in double-checking a few things in construction more conveniently and efficiently. Disaster and safety management are easily performed with the engineers' ease and with the more detailed use of drones. Firms require regular safety inspections of water pipelines, powerlines, gas pipelines, and bridge foundations. Crop monitoring, agricultural surveying, and geological mapping are anticipated to benefit as well from drone technology. Installing a camera and preprogramming the flight path are required and the drones do the remaining work, which is an easy way to measure specific locations.
  • Assisting Scientific Research and Journalists - Scientists involved in the research of hazardous locations such as areas with active volcanoes or places that emit fumes are anticipated to study seismic activity more in detail with drone usage. Wildlife conservationists and researchers extensively use drones t to research wildlife of all kinds. Drone usage is effective in researching dangerous world animal activity and assists researchers in surveying the habitat and looking for problems such as illegal deforestation and poaching. Drones are potential allies in preventing endangered animal extinction and preserving nature. Moreover, journalists are anticipated to leverage the advantages of drones for easy research in remote areas to offer detailed information on situations such as remote location terrain or make videos of places that are far more dangerous to move across. Drone usage cuts costs, which is the prime advantage offered by drone technology, and a few tasks that required a helicopter previously are now done by drones such as recording localized areas. Therefore, drones assist in reducing the human labor and costs of operating and hiring the helicopter.
  • Enabling Easy Delivery - Drone-based delivery service is poised to become a prominent application of the technology, especially for areas that are difficult to access through roads. The use of drones for delivery pivots on efficiency as they are effective for areas with high traffic. In addition, drone technology plays an important role in reducing carbon emissions and road accidents. Online food ordering & delivery services allow restaurants to reduce physical locations as well as save on real estate expenses. Various restaurants are also adopting drones for cheaper, faster delivery to cut delivery costs and improve profitability. Drones are capable of holding a parcel easily, based on the payload attached, and delivery firms and online stores are developing solutions related to drone usage. The usage of drones to deliver products is anticipated to make the job of product delivery easy, keep people safe, and totally transform the delivery sector in years to come. At present, a number of drone delivery services are being tested in several countries. Examples include Amazon’s drone delivery system, Domino’s flying drone for pizza delivery, Lakemaid Beer drone delivery, Google’s Project Wing in Australia, DHL in Germany, and a drone-based delivery network being tested in Bhutan.
  • Assisting Emergency Responders in Saving Lives - Emergency response units and healthcare services are anticipated to use drones to assist patients more efficiently and quickly if the situation demands because of their ability to make delivery easy. Rescue operations demand that rescue personnel arrive quickly on the scene, as delays in rescue operations lead to severe repercussions. The relief goods delivery to the rescue location becomes possible with the drone usage to provide emergency assistance like items related to first aid and blood donation to rescue a person's life. Healthcare services are making development in drone usage to decrease human labor, reduce time consumption in rescue operations, and make the delivery of items highly precise by upgrading drone technology and drones. Drones with night vision or thermal sensor cameras are possibly used to locate people who are lost in the dark. Furthermore, the drones' ability to capture videos is useful in reporting to police suspicious activities, potentially saving lives.
  • Applications in Military - Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), referring to remote controlled or autonomously piloted aircrafts, have been a major force in modern-day military applications and battlefield scenarios. With early generations of drones characterized by their large size and high cost, military agencies were the only ones to possess the specialized skills needed to operate these autonomous machines. The military benefited immensely from drone technology, and drones are possibly utilized for military purposes such as air strikes, bomb detection, and spying. The most prevalent drones by the military are for airstrikes and are called UAVs, and the drones are possibly equipped with bombs and missiles to launch airstrikes. The UAV technology is utilized by the US military to bomb enemy hideouts and territories without risking the life of their own soldiers. The small size of several drones enables them to infiltrate small spaces with reduced risk of detection by enemies. Drones are utilized to detonate bombs as an enemy distraction and life-saving tactic or as the detector by attaching the camera on it. Drones are utilized by military surveillance and are possibly equipped with a night or thermal vision sensors and cameras if situations call for unique equipment. The UAVs are capable of acting like spies to infiltrate the bases and count enemy numbers in specific locations. Keeping a nation and its citizens safe while reducing the risk to soldiers is the main advantage of drone usage by the military.
  • Video Recording and Photo Capturing - Drones have emerged as a compelling recreational tool for outdoor settings, supporting various activities such as extreme sports footage and aerial landscape photography. The technology assists producers in capturing appealing aerial perspectives without using helicopters, enabling cinematography to attain new heights, saving costs and improving bottom lines. Drone usage for taking videos and photos takes the cool factor of posting great videos on social media further. Drones are possibly used to capture the entire neighborhood or capture fantastic images of nature from the bird's eye view. The ability of drones to capture images of high-value properties makes them a suitable option for the real estate industry. In addition to using cost-effective aerial photography for taking pictures of exteriors, participants are relying on agile, small UAVs for capturing home interiors.
  • Architectural firms- Are increasingly using drones for getting footage and images of a property for creating 3D renderings of structures intended to be built. Architects are creating real-world concepts of projects by quickly and cost-effectively creating desirable aerial shots for placing architectural rendering. These aspects allow creation of accurate designs as well as help users understand how these elements fit in properties.
  • Urban Planning - With rapid urbanization, cities are required to adapt to chronic congestion and larger populations that highlights the significance of urban planning and approaches to understand a metropolitan. Drones allow urban planners to understand the environment as well as implement data-based improvements for efficient infrastructure. Consulting firms are leveraging drones for collecting data in densely populated areas to help users in understanding the urban habitat. Drones provide budget-constraint municipalities with an affordable option to capture urban data. The technology also assists city planners in identifying areas that can benefit from green space without increasing the congestion.
  • Mapping with UAV Drones - Drones intended for mapping are equipped with powerful sensors to provide bird-eye view of an area and gather data. Mapping and associated data allows growers to assess crop health, monitor crops and avail insurance services. While health assessment focuses on detecting plant diseases and identifying health issues, crop monitoring is intended to track changes in crop. The rising demand for drones in mapping application has prompted participants in the market to come with innovative drones and sophisticated software. While leading players are offering drones equipped with mapping software and sensors, various startups are foraying into the market with advanced platforms. The trend is also driving various companies to forge partnerships and join hands with mapping service and software providers.

How things would play out in 2023 and beyond will be largely driven by certain key trends in the area of corporate diversity, in addition to the actions that leaders would take around these trends.


  • Growth in Commercial and Government Use - Agricultural applications and infrastructure are expected to lead growth in the adoption of drones in commercial applications. UAVs are crucial for sustaining the aging infrastructure of a nation, ranging from inspecting pipelines and bridges to prioritizing critical infrastructure repair after natural disasters. Drones can also play a significant role in combating the impacts of climate change, providing additional tools to farmers to monitor water requirements and crop health, and ensuring better coordination among firefighters during wildfire response efforts.
  • Aerial LiDAR - In the last few years, Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) technology has witnessed substantial growth in adoption. LiDAR is primarily a method of detecting objects and measuring distances in real space by sending down laser pulses to the surface and measuring the time required by the pulse to return to the sensor. LiDAR technology is used extensively to gather data on various surfaces and develop a three-dimensional (3D) model. However, the rising availability of LiDAR sensors has increased the application of these sensors in new use cases. Additionally, the mainstreaming of drone technology has led to its convergence with LiDAR technology. Traditionally, LiDAR has been utilized by satellites, planes, or helicopters for mapping the ground below. However, drone LiDAR is gradually revolutionizing the aerial mapping industry and is expected to be utilized on a significant scale in the upcoming years. Currently, drones have made LiDAR more accessible to organizations and businesses in different sectors such as mining, construction, archaeology, and environmental sciences.
  • Autonomous Drones Driven by Algorithms - The growing popularity and mainstreaming of autonomous technology have increased the prospect of autonomous drones in near future. Both machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) are anticipated to play a crucial role in manufacturing different autonomous drones that can fly without any manual intervention. The combination of drone technology with AI significantly improves efficiency and performance by enabling drones to make their own decisions and reduce their reliance on human operators. The combination of AI and advanced software offers analytical reports that can be used to diagnose crop health. While some of the leading players have launched fully-automated drones, others are using drones with inbuilt hyperspectral sensors for capturing aerial images of land, analyze data with the help of AI, and offer information pertaining to surveying, inspection, surveillance and monitoring. Autonomous drones are expected to assist logistics businesses to a significant extent as these drones can eliminate manual errors and operate much faster in a systematic manner. Last-mile logistics is anticipated to witness a fundamental transformation, with autonomous drones sending packages within minutes in place of hours.
  • Use of Drones in Supply Chains - Amazon is deploying drones extensively for autonomous deliveries. Several other companies are also expected to increase the use of drones due to rapid growth in online deliveries and e-commerce and the shortage of delivery drivers.
  • Monitoring the Sources of Renewable Energy - Climate change has currently become an issue of global concern and is playing a significant role in shaping future policies. Rising focus on renewable energy has encouraged several companies to enter into renewable energy generation. However, identifying and mapping an area suitable for an energy plan is extremely challenging. Drones can substantially assist in mapping and surveying large areas to identify a suitable area for renewable energy sources, such as wind turbines or solar panels. Additionally, drones with thermographic imaging capabilities can constantly monitor such areas.
  • Solar Farm Monitoring - Solar energy is expected to be adopted on a significant scale in the upcoming years, necessitating the construction of multiple solar farms. However, the operation and maintenance of these solar farms in geographically remote and dispersed areas can be extremely challenging. Autonomous drones possessing thermographic imaging capabilities can be used for round-the-clock monitoring of such farms for temperature analysis, possible malfunctions, and maintenance.
  • Drones in the Management of Oil and Gas Facilities - Oil and gas facilities can be monitored effectively using drones to ensure plant and environmental safety. Specifically, autonomous on-site industrial drone systems can be utilized to monitor and inspect dangerous places and hard-to-reach areas to ensure the safety of workers. Additionally, drones can effectively and efficiently detect anomalies such as temperature surges and leaks to prevent disasters or provide an immediate response to disasters.
  • Enhancing Protection and Surveillance - Drones can be used extensively for surveillance, threat prediction, and data collection. Law enforcement firms, border patrols, and paramilitary groups use drones to combat various criminal activities. Drone activities can also play a crucial role in military use cases such as fugitive tracking, hostage situation handling, surveillance, and bomb threats.
  • Ensuring the Safety of Ports - Port terminal operators can monitor surrounding coastlines, port exits and entrances, and freight and crane operators using autonomous industrial drone systems. Such drones can detect, alert, and track suspicious activities automatically, which eliminates the requirement for human supervision or puts human staff at risk for similar purposes.
  • Reduced Premiums on Insurance - Replacing humans with drones for risky activities, such as repairs, maintenance, and inspections, can significantly decrease general liability insurance premiums for companies, specifically utilities. For instance, high-voltage power lines can be inspected thoroughly and safely using flying cameras in place of employing a lineman who has to sit on the skids of a helicopter for inspection. Drones are also anticipated to be used in other sectors, such as the construction sector, which can benefit from the cost and time savings required during visual site inspections.
  • Drones in Digital-twins - A virtual model or a close-to-exact/scaled replica of a physical object with a scaled dimension and size, wall thickness, temperature, and the color is referred to as Digital-Twin. Digital twins are extremely critical for the infrastructure sector as these twins are used extensively before the construction of large-scale properties. Drones can make more efficient digital twins as they can capture detailed overviews/360-degree footage of physical assets, even those that are difficult to reach, at a close range, and can easily and quickly deliver the data necessary to develop the digital twin with extreme accuracy using optimum energy.
  • Drone Journalism - International and local news organizations have often relied on traffic cameras and helicopters to report car accidents and traffic conditions. However, several new channels are increasingly using drones for capturing the required footage as drones can fly much lower than helicopters and provide detailed information about disaster situations and road accidents, leading to time, resource, and cost savings at the same time.
