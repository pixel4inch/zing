WHAT'S TRENDING

The field of nanotechnology is evolving rapidly with considerable potential for advancement and application in various areas. Huge investments are being made by companies and governments around the world for R&D in this field. In the mainstream nanotechnology at present, modern approaches are being used to create miniature particles and then applying them in various areas. Some of the products that now include nanoparticles are sunscreens, certain paints, some water- and stain-repellent clothing, some types of anti-fogging and anti-repellent glass, and certain cosmetics, among others. Home projectors, inkjet printers, cell phones, and air bags are some of the products that have gained from the nanotechnology available today. MEMS (microelectromechanical systems), which are used in various electronic products have become smaller over the years, now being as tiny as a rice grain. They enable phones, smartwatches, and such others to identify their orientation, and the direction and magnitude of their acceleration.


Sensors of such size enable air bags to deploy at the right time. Further, Metalens (a latest nanomachine) have demonstrated (in lab) to have the capability to influence light in a manner that would otherwise need the use of several traditional lenses. This technology is starting to find use in commercial applications. One among them is the production of 3D sensors for smartphones. This use of metalens will enable phone producers to attain the type of 3D sensing that the Face ID technology of Apple facilitates. Also, as Metalenses allow the identification of polarized light, they could sense things such as light pollution, black ice by automobile cameras, and enable cameras on phones to detect skin cancer. But for reducing the size of nanomachines and achieving the goal of greater miniaturization (theoretical) to the extent at which individual atoms can be manipulated, novel and more advanced technologies would be required. The pharmaceutical industry is focusing on finding means to enhance drug delivery using nanotechnology. More applications of nanotechnology are likely to emerge in the future.


Despite the immediate and near-term challenges, long-term fundamentals for growth of nanomaterials, nanodevices and nanotools remains strong. The value offered by nanotechnology in improving efficiency, productivity, safety of processes and quality and durability of finished products remains unchanged by the ongoing pandemic and economic/financial scenario. As the fourth industrial revolution moves from automated to autonomous, the role nanotechnology is poised to get bigger, and more important. In the post COVID-19 period, use of nanotechnology solutions in the production of a multitude of devices & products will continue to grow. A return to normalcy will bring back growth in end-use sectors such as semiconductors & electronics; biotechnology; chemicals; pharmaceutical & healthcare; aerospace & defense; automotive; food industry; construction; agriculture; telecom; paints & coatings among others. The ever-present demand for slimmer and lighter consumer electronics; efficient semiconductor fabrication methods; more potent drugs and medical devices; lighter yet stronger materials and components for aerospace, defense, automotive sectors; and smart wrappings/packaging for the food and pharma industries, will drive recovery of growth in the year 2021. New and emerging applications in energy, construction, textiles, agriculture, environment protection, cosmetics and sporting goods augur well for the future of nanotechnology.


Use of flexible nanoparticles in cosmetic products; application of nanocoating in mobile devices, and use of silver nanotubes for water purifiers, dust, stain or water proof resistant textiles indicates expanding opportunities for nanotechnology beyond the traditional end-use segments to new and emerging market segments. Potential nanotech opportunities will also arise from telecom and information technology industries. With a line-up of already successful products and many more to be commercialized in coming years, there is still plenty of room for commercial expansion of nanotechnology in coming years. Recovering focus on R&D activity and increasing number of products that will be commercialized will help lend significant growth traction to the market in coming years. With many nanotechnology based products currently progressing from research laboratories to manufacturing process, the stage is all set for future growth. Increasing production capacities and subsequent decline in their prices will also help drive growth in the nanotechnology products market in coming years. Over the last few years, the market witnessed a significant drop in prices of its broad gamut of products such as nanoclays and carbon nanotubes, thus helping broaden application possibilities of nanotechnology in wide spectrum of industries. Rising number of production facilities and increasing number of companies foraying into the market only augurs well for the future of nanotechnology market. While the United States and Europe continue to remain major geographic markets for nanotechnology, developing markets such as Asia-Pacific are now taking center stage both in research and development and commercialization of nanotechnology products.


  • Electronic and Electronic - Nanocomposites find robust adoption in electronic and electronic applications given their high thermal resistance and electrical conductivity. Electronic segment is one of the fastest growing end-use markets for nanocomposites driving robust gains in the market. Semiconductor sector is also attracted by the electrically conductive properties of polymer nanocomposites. Besides electronic and electrical, nanocomposites also serves several other end-use markets including building & construction, automotive, IT, energy, and packaging where they have several commercialized and potential applications.
  • Construction - The construction industry is already exploring nanocomposite materials to enhance the strength of structures as well as to ensure effective insulation. New composite materials such as aerogel, owing to their excellent insulation characteristics are growing in demand.
  • Plastics - Strong opportunities are expected to also stem from the plastics industry wherein small addition of these nanocomposites to different polymers via in-situ polymerization or melt compounding more than doubles the heat resistance, electrical conductivity and dimensional stability of plastics, thus expanding its use. For instance, clay nanocomposites, and nanocarbon fillers are being used in the manufacture of plastic bottles to enhance the shelf life of beverages like beer.
  • Automotive & Aerospace - The automotive & aerospace industries also are major revenue generating end-use application areas for nanocomposites. Polymer nanocomposite, as a new class of multiphase materials, will especially witness sturdy gains driven by the growing focus of auto majors on reducing fuel consumption through reduction in vehicle weight, enhancement of engine efficiency and reduction in vehicular emissions. Future application areas span the use of polymer nanocomposites in the manufacture of door inners, body panels, head lamp covers, engine covers, and windows, among others. Elastomeric nanocomposites will find increased use in the manufacture of tires. Traditionally aerospace and automotive designers have always grappled with power-weight ratio issues. While heavier payloads demand access to more powerful engines, aerospace experts always strive to design and develop lighter spacecrafts. Automotive engineers strive hard to minimize vehicular weight and attain optimal power-weight ratio in their vehicles. Traditionally aerospace designers have extensively used carbon fiber composites. However the increasing need to launch heavier communication satellites, international space station modules payloads into orbits has prompted engineers to consider lighter and stronger alternatives. Against this backdrop, nanocomposite materials have garnered specific interest.
  • Food & Beverage Packaging - Favorable government regulations encouraging the use of nanocomposites in food & beverage packaging are also expected to influence growth in the market in coming years. Rising demand for high-performance bio-based nanocomposites such as cellulose nanocrystal-filled polyurethane (PU), bacterial cellulose with polyvinyl alcohol (PVOH), polysulphone/cellulose nanocomposites, and cellulose nanocrystal-filled carboxymethyl cellulose is also expected to drive growth in the nanocomposites market over the next few years. While high production cost is a currently major challenge for nanocomposite manufacturing, technology developments especially those optimizing production techniques will go a long way in reducing cost of production, lowing product costs and ensuring sustainability in coming the next few years.


  • Evolving Role of Nanoelectronics in Emerging Era of AI, Autonomous Systems, IoT & 5G -
    The emerging era of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Internet of Things (IoT), Autonomous Systems, and 5G communication networks is poised to spur the opportunities for nanoelectronics. Nanoelectronic devices have already shown great promise in an array of electronic solutions including quantum computers, memory devices, optoelectronic devices, display systems, radio equipment, energy production systems, medical diagnostics and many others. Various approaches for nanoelectronics include nanofabrication, nanomaterials electronics, and molecular electronics. Nanomaterial electronics are small and enable more number of transistors to be integrated onto a single chip. Further, the symmetrical and uniform structure of nanotubes or nanowires allows greater electron mobility, symmetrical electron/hole characteristic, and higher dielectric constant. Nanoparticles also hold potential usage as quantum dots. Active involvement of industry players is contributing immensely to growth and progress of nanoelectronics market.

  • Nanotechnology Gains Traction in Electronics -
    Nanomaterials are being incorporated into a variety of electronic devices to improve their performance. The unique optical-electronics properties of nanomaterials facilitates its use in applications such as batteries, solar cells, sensors, electronic conductors, computer chips, non-volatile memories, flat panel displays, optoelectronics, and molecular sized transistors and switches among others. With the rise of miniaturization in electronics industry, nanomaterials are important components in chip design. Nanomaterials are being used to connect resistors, conductors, and other elements of an electronic chip. They are designed for use as conductors from printable inks to electronic chips.
  • The Quest for Lightweight Fuel Efficient Cars to Spur Use of Nanotechnology in Automotive Applications
    The global automotive nanomaterials market is heading for phenomenal expansion through 2025 due to appealing properties of these materials over conventional options. In the coming years, growing demand for new generation automobiles featuring newer and lighter materials will extend a rich mixture of opportunities and challenges for nanotechnology in automotive applications. New generation materials capable of handling high performance and specific requirements for lighter materials are poised to score the highest gains in the upcoming years.     The application of nanomaterials enables automakers to reduce vehicle weight for enhanced fuel economy and low carbon emissions. These materials are witnessing increasing adoption in the automotive industry to push efficiency and extend product life. Leading automakers are increasingly betting on these materials to achieve lightweight engines and developing better tires with low rolling resistance and excellent grip. In addition, nanomaterial use in lithium-ion batteries plays an important role in reducing recharge time. The automotive nanomaterials market is anticipated to receive strong stimulus from increasing influx of sophisticated material options including nanodispersions, nanoadditives and nanocatalysts with potential applications in coolants, lubricants and fuels.
  • Growing Demand for Anti-Corrosion & Anti-Fingerprint Coatings to Spur Demand for Nanocoatings
    Anti-corrosion nanocoatings continue to rank among some of the most widely used nanocoatings in the world, thanks to their versatility and environmental and cost benefits. In line with growing concerns about corrosion of materials in industrial equipment, plants, and installations due to extreme conditions such as aqueous environment, anti-corrosion nanocoatings are gaining attention in the industrial sector due to their efficiency. Nanotechnology based anti-corrosion coatings with self-healing characteristics offer environmental benefits and rank as the only feasible alternative to toxic chromium coatings. Nanocoatings with anti-corrosion features are the new generation of protective coating systems. Also, direct and indirect costs associated with corrosion of metallic structures and products currently represent a huge economic burden, an added commercial incentive for further research in nanotechnology based protective coatings. The use of nanomaterials-based coating is also growing due to the fact that it increases the lifecycle of metallic structures.

    Demand for anti-corrosion coatings will continue to be driven by growing usage on a range of surfaces including metal, brass, copper, steel, plastic, aluminum, rubber, ceramics, chrome, titanium, polymers, paint, magnesium, and bronze. These coatings are finding applications in oil & gas, energy, shipping & shipyard, infrastructure, lifting equipment, rolling stock, port machinery, and lighting sectors. Major benefits driving growth in the market include increased coating durability; high insulation; no permeability to oxygen and water; high adhesion to metal surface; absolute continuity of coating film; and high mechanical strength. These coatings with excellent adhesion properties offer resistance from scratch, UV, weathering, bacteria & mold, water, fungus, blistering, and dirt, which are boosting their usage.

  • Nanomaterials Transform the Construction Sector-
    The construction industry of late has begun to explore nanocomposite materials to enhance the strength of structures as well as to ensure effective insulation. New composite materials such as aerogel, owing to their excellent insulation characteristics are growing in demand. Driven by the need to design and build durable, safer and taller structures and skyscrapers, construction engineers are always exploring possibilities of incorporating innovative new materials to offer enhanced maintainability, durability and structural strength. Against this backdrop, nanotechnology is witnessing strong growth as a technology capable of improving material properties and functions, and enhancing energy efficiency and conservation. The technology flaunts the potential to significantly reduce energy consumed by heating, lighting, and air conditioning. Nanotitanium oxide particles owing to its impressive weather resistant properties are being used in window nanocoating applications to impart self-cleaning capabilities. Nanoparticles impregnated paints are also being used for weather proofing applications. While fiber wrapping concrete has been around for a while, nanofiber reinforced concrete, owing to its much higher strength is eliciting heightened attention over the last few years. Nanoparticles comprising of TiO2, Al2O3 or ZnO find applications in construction ceramics to develop tiles, glass and sanitaryware that are resistant UV light and impervious to dirt and pollution. Construction materials like cement, asphalt, concrete, steel, coatings and paints, are fortified with nano silica (silica fume), nanostructured metals, iron oxide nanoparticles, copper nanoparticles, silver nanoparticles, quantum dots, silicon dioxide nanoparticles, titanium dioxide nanoparticles, carbon nanotubes (CNTs) and carbon nanofibers (CNFs) in a bid to develop self-healing, and resilient structures. These materials improve mechanical/compressive strength, provide crack and abrasion resistance to concrete, enhance weldability & corrosion resistance of steel, impart fire-resistant characteristics to ceramics, and extend anti-microbial capabilities to paints and coatings.

    Presently, food manufacturers and research groups are actively endeavoring to apply these advances to the food industry. Initial efforts primarily revolved around use of the technology in the area of food packaging but swift progress is being witnessed in the area of nanoparticles and nanoencapsulation techniques to deliver required bioactive compounds to intended areas of the body. One of the major applications of nanotechnology within the food sector comprises food processing that is usually carried out for the development of nano-textured/nano-structured food products including ice creams, mayonnaises, yoghurts and creams. Nano-texturing of food materials is known to provide improved texture, stability and consistency of emulsions and enhanced taste in comparison to other forms of normal processed food products. In addition, nano-textured food products are also considered amongst the healthiest forms especially due to their low-fat content. Nano-encapsulated food additives are growing in popularity in more areas than nano-textured or nano-structured food stuffs that predominantly assist in enhancing textures, stability and consistency of emulsions. Nano-encapsulated food additives feature higher surface area when compared to conventional forms and thereby aid in imparting better flavors and tastes. Several forms of nano-sized additives are available in the market across various sectors such as food, nutraceutical and food supplement sectors in the form of antioxidants, vitamins, antimicrobials and minerals.
  • in Nanotechnology Explodes in Agriculture
    Nanotechnology appears poised to be a big breather for the agricultural and food systems that are struggling with negative implications of climate change and degradation of the ecosystem. The use of nanotechnology holds significant potential to pave way for sustainable agriculture owing to its ability to restore or improve soil fertility and quality while boosting crop production. The idea of nanotechnology can be exploited to support various aspects of the agricultural system, including nano-pesticide delivery and transport of desired genetic materials to stimulate crop development. The technology is used for controlled, slow release of bio-fertilizer containing nanoparticles. In addition, nanotechnology is enabling development of advanced nano biosensors that allow faster detection of biotic/abiotic stress and phytopathogens.
    Researchers have developed a number of nanoparticles for commercial use in the agricultural ecosystem. For instance, the application of polymeric nanoparticles allows delivery of sustainable agrochemicals in a controlled manner. The growing popularity of polymeric nanoparticles can be attributed to their low impact on the wildlife in soil and their biocompatibility. Some of the commonly used polymeric nanomaterials in the field of agriculture include polyethylene glycol, poly(lactide-co-glycolides), poly(epsilon-caprolactone), and poly (γ-glutamic acid). On the other hand, silver nanoparticles are finding increasing use due to their desired antimicrobial properties against various phytopathogens and the ability to stimulate plant growth. While nano alumino-silicate formulations present efficient pesticides, titanium dioxide nanoparticles offer a biocompatible option and can be applied as disinfecting agents for water. In addition, carbon nanomaterials are being increasingly used to improve the seed germination process. Other nanoparticles that are gaining broader attention in the agriculture industry are copper oxide nanoparticles, zinc oxide and magnetic nanoparticles. These growing applications and uses of nanoparticles and nanomaterials are anticipated to pave way for sustainable farming.

  • Nanomaterials Gather Pace in Paints & Coatings Industry
    Growing demand for better quality coatings have always encouraged manufacturers to explore several new advanced technologies in order to improve their product performance and bring out new advanced coating varieties. Paints and coatings are increasingly using resins, binders, adhesives and pigments made of nanomaterials. Nanomaterials are used as additives to enhance the rheology of paints and inks. Products are available for both aqueous and organic solvent based systems. As nanoparticles provide a larger surface area and quantum effects, they can provide better coating properties.   • Growing Use of Nanomaterials in Water Treatment Applications Presents Favorable Opportunities
    Wastewater contaminants, including toxic heavy metals and other impurities, cause multiple adverse effects on living beings and the environment. Nanotechnology has proved to be an efficient and less expensive tool for industrial water treatment. The unique properties of nanomaterials help in the removal of heavy metals from polluted wastewater. Nanomaterials such as carbon nanotubes (CNTs), silver nanoparticles, nanoparticles of zero valent iron (ZVI), zeolites and others find application in water remediation, while polymeric nanoparticles are used for wastewater treatment. Nanotechnology-based water purification devices have the potential to transform the field of desalination. The new nanofiltration technology can be used in water treatment applications in homes, offices, and industries. Iron nanoparticles promises potential in effectively cleaning up organic solvents that are polluting groundwater. This method can be more cost-effective than treatment methods that require the water to be pumped out of the ground. Layered nanomaterials comprising graphene and graphene oxide, MoS2, and MXenes are used extensively in industrial water treatment.
