Factors Fostering Startup Culture in Israel


  • Favorable Policy Framework - Government policies and initiatives are playing a crucial role in emergence of Israel as one of the main powerhouses in the field of science and technology. Israel maintains a consistent focus on significant investments in education and support immigration for strengthening human capital. The government has created a favorable framework to support new businesses while announcing various policy initiatives like R&D programs, network institutions and tax-related frameworks. The government is offering investment incentives to attract foreign investors and making notable investments in R&D, which is higher than the investment level in other industrialized nations. In addition, the government has implemented venture capital programs for successful conversion of research and innovative ideas into reality. Public policies in the country have led to a business-friendly landscape that is fostering innovations and supporting initiatives taken by the private sector through financing, regulatory framework, education and infrastructure. The government offers university research and technology transfer programs, incubators and subsidized venture capital. The strong focus of Israel on R&D can be credited to factors such as scarcity of land and natural resources, few avenues for national growth and the political landscape that limits most businesses to neighboring nations. These limitations are prompting Israel to focus on the human capital along with innovative solutions that can be sold on the international market.
  • Strong Focus on Education - The thriving entrepreneurial culture in Israel is credited to its unwavering attention on education along with the cultural focus on factors such as risk-taking and self-initiative. The lack of natural resources in the country has resulted in strong focus on approaches to push human capital and value-added products and services. Israel is making significant investments into education, with its half of the workforce holding the tertiary education level. The education network in Israel encompasses schools and universities along with the military, where all citizens are required to engage in for two or more years. The military provides people with advanced knowledge related to technology and engineering. The factor has enabled Israel to surpass countries such as the US and Germany in terms of the number of engineers per capita. In addition, the country makes notable investment in R&D activity, leading to large number of full-time researchers, technical and scientific journal articles. Moreover, Israel focuses on value-added manufacturing, with a major fraction of manufacturing exports from the country representing high-tech products.
  • Mandatory Military Service - The mandatory military service for all people above the age of 18 is one of the key proponents of entrepreneurship in Israel. Some of the renowned founders of companies in the country have served 8200, the Israeli Intelligence Corps unit dedicated to collect signal intelligence and code decryption. The 8200 is no more a secretive unit and shares its proficiency by becoming a highly popular pool for organizations looking for bright people in specific areas of technology such as engineering and communications. The Israeli Defense Force has also produced a large number of CEOs and founders. In addition, women in the country serve all positions on the military equally and promoted on the basis of merit instead of gender. The significant contribution of the military in fostering the entrepreneurship in the country is attributed to the fact that the service provides young women and men with advanced technological training. Israeli military selects people on the basis of merit and trains them at next-generation computing units, assigning young engineers with the responsibility to manage challenging projects. The military environment is intentionally designed to ensure minimal guidance from seniors for compelling young soldiers to improvise and deal with challenges to compete assigned tasks. The environment is playing an important role in creating youngsters who learn from failures and can take chances. Moreover, the process results in innovative and disciplined professionals that can work hard and take risks.
  • Unique Culture - Israeli is a unique blend of Jewish, American, European and sub-ethnic Levantine cultures and customs. The country has witnessed influx of Jewish people from the US, Europe, Australia, Asia and Africa over the past century. The modern society in Israel is a fusion of Western and Eastern influences that have led to a shared and unique culture. In addition, Israelis are known for their risk-taking, openness and individualism along with the ability to think freely, debate, speak out and question the status quo. Majority of businesses in the country seek individuals who are risk takers and independent thinkers, which holds particular relevance for the high-tech field that is commonly associated with risk taking. There are various entrepreneurs in Israel who come up with new ventures, push them and eventually sell them before working on another venture. In addition, nearly one-fifth of the country’s population is represented by Christians and Muslims that are working everywhere, from the parliament and healthcare system through to industry and academia. The cultural diversity is notably contributing to innovations and the startup culture.
  • Immigration - The strong human capital in Israel is attributed to favorable immigration policies that welcome Jews from different countries. A large number of these immigrants are marked by high ambition, technical know-how, advanced degrees, focus on education and unique approaches to problems. Accounting for a decent share of Israeli population, Soviet Jews who immigrated to the country during the 1980s were leading in diverse fields such as mathematics, science and engineering. These immigrants along with their children are contributing to immense success enjoyed by the country. In addition, Israel is attracting skilled people from different countries such as the US, the UK, Canada, France and Australia. The noteworthy influx of skilled and highly ambitious professionals has helped Israel in scoring high in terms of entrepreneurship-related initiatives. These immigrants are not only bringing high-tech skills, but also enabling the country to connect to well-established international markets and network for success.
  • Business & Financial Climate - The strong focus of Israel on R&D investment and innovations has resulted in proliferation of researchers, enabling the country to boast the highest share of researchers. The education system and the military have led to a vibrant business culture in the country with impressive work ethic. Business culture in Israel is characterized by the attitude to risk taking and acceptance of failure as a part of innovation, a key component of the startup culture. While closely-tied community allows startups to find partners, clustered population and short geographical distances further support businesses. In addition, Israel is gaining from a positive financial climate marked by high confidence of investors in the country. Israel score over key countries such as Canada, Australia, Japan and China in terms of investor confidence. The scenario is attributed to the presence of a favorable business environment, little risk and better prospects. Israel also boasts the highest level of R&D investment globally as a fraction of the GDP, leading to strong knowledge base and business developments. These factors are enabling Israeli startups to secure the major fraction of investments from foreign players.
  • Scarcity of Natural Resources - The abundance of natural resources in a country is perceived to hinder economic growth and political modernization. On the other hand, countries with no to limited natural resources are required to develop survival characteristics, strive for becoming resourceful, and explore new approaches to do things in an optimized way. These traits align with crucial characteristics of startups that are marked by few resources and must come up with innovative ways for business expansion. Barring the recent gas discoveries, Israel had long been relying on export of gas and development of alternative energy sources. In addition, the country has been dealing with draught since its early years. The issue prompted Israel to aggressively push desalination method for overcoming the persistent challenge. The technology has enabled Israel to emerge as a water-independent nation. Water security has emerged as one of the promising domains in the country and is anticipated to account for a notable share in exports in the coming years. In addition, the country is expected to export the water technology to other regions for dealing with drought.
  • Focus on Self-Reliance - Characterized by scarcity of natural resources and numerous enemies, Israel maintains a strong focus on self-reliance for survival. Since there are bleak opportunities for normalization with neighboring countries and cross-border trade, Israel follows a self-preservation approach. Startup founders in the country bet high on technological innovations to taste success and survive in the highly competitive global market. Despite holding a number of trading partners in the US and Europe, Israel faces a risk of economic sanctions and trade embargo. The scenario prompts the country to aggressively push innovation and create value-added products. In addition, startups are receiving significant support from the government in terms of human capital development and research efforts for moving ahead in the international market.
