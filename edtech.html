WHAT'S TRENDING

Powered by EduTech, the education sector is walking aplomb into the digital era with impressive grades and creating endless learning opportunities for students to get ready for the environment that is evolving exponentially rather than linearly. The emergence of Industry 4.0 and pressing requirement of technology-driven skills are prompting the education system to embrace the change. Growing familiarity of children with advanced digital systems and gadgets also makes them ready for digital education and points towards significance of education technology. EduTech holds tremendous potential to accelerate transition of the education industry from conventional teaching approaches towards modern ways for improved teaching and learning experiences.


The use of advanced technology is paving way for better education content and teaching models, which improve engagement and help students in grasping concepts in a new and exciting way. With digital transformation being the future of education domain, an increasing number of educational institutions are drifting towards next-generation technologies for enhanced learning outcomes. The use of technology in the field of education is increasing rapidly, paving way for replacement of textbooks, chalk and blackboards with technology-based solutions. Educators are embracing technology and latest tools owing to their ability to engage students within physical and online classrooms. Encompassing AR and VR, extended reality is a major EduTech trend that allows immersive and engaging learning environments for students. These technologies are being exploited by universities and educational institutions to develop virtual laboratories for science and technology students for hands-on learning. Cloud computing is anticipated to hold a major role in supporting EduTech in K-12 education.


The education landscape is witnessing strong focus on micro- and nano-learning approaches that involve bite-sized courses or lessons for K-12 or higher education. Wearable technology is another factor with profound implications for the education system as it allows students to learn various concepts using their bracelets, smart watches and smart glasses. Digital assessments are garnering increasing attention from educational institutions. The approach takes student assessments beyond theoretical exams and eliminates bias. In the coming years, the education system is anticipated to witness increasing acceptance of non-theoretical, field and experimental education on the expense of conventional examinations. These developments are poised to transform the education system and attract various companies and startups to serve the domain with advanced technologies. EduTech is set to radically transform the education domain and play a crucial role in preparing students for the digital future.


Discussed below are the many ways in which technology is changing education –


  • Easy Access to Information- Increasing reliance of students on technology is prompting educational institutions to integrate education technology into existing workflows to aid easy access to information and resources. The familiarity of students with digital technology and mobile devices makes it convenient for them to stay engagement in classes. The availability of search engines, primarily Google, has reduced the need for learners to visit libraries by enabling access to digital information. The learning environment is rapidly turning into online environment and enabling flow of desired information into different formats such as text, video, audio and images. These formats help students in better understanding the teaching material for enhanced learning.
  • >Online Learning - The adoption of advanced communication technologies has paved way for broader acceptance of online learning. The teaching approach gained notably during the pandemic that led to temporarily shutdown of schools and other educational institutions, prompting them to go embrace the online way. The concept of online education was already popular before the crisis as it provides people with the flexibility and opportunity to pursue specific courses for professional development in an affordable manner. In addition, online education allows people to pursue education or courses from any geographical location. In the recent years, an increasing number of educational institutions have started leveraging digital technology to help students in joining classes, taking tests and working on projects as per their convenience. The move is poised to help people in continuing their leaning journey and accessing quality education.
  • Modernized Classrooms - Education technology is expediting transition of classrooms away from conventional approaches such as blackboard or classroom globe towards smart gadgets and advanced technology. The integration of smart boards and other technologies like artificial intelligence and virtual reality is making classrooms truly smarter and modern. Virtual reality is enabling students to virtually visit any location on the earth for better grasp over concepts related to geography. These advances align with the digital world and help educational institutions in improving learning outcomes.
  • Better Learning Experience - The incorporation of modern technologies is and enabling teachers to develop innovative and creative lesson plans for ensuring enhanced engagement and the learning experience for students in real or virtual classrooms. EduTech is playing a pivotal role in enhancing individual learning, removing geographical barriers and revolving issues faced by teachers. Education technology offers different tools, course plans and learning styles that help students in understanding even complex topics. In addition, the inclusion of technology in classrooms is slated to prepare students for the digital world by making them familiar with new digital technologies.
  • Breaking down Long-Standing Barriers - Education is increasingly becoming more accessible and flexible with rising adoption of digital technology, a move that is overcoming challenges such as geographical barriers and expensive nature of education. Representing indispensable component of EduTech trend, mobile and online learning are removing physical boundaries. Moreover, options like e-learning have played an important role in significantly reducing the cost of education by eliminating the requirement for students to purchase textbooks. Online learning platforms are enabling students to continue their education, even from another nation or without leaving home. EduTech is opening opportunities students to enroll in desired programs from top universities. In addition, an increasing number of working professionals are embracing the approach for further education.

  • AI & Adaptive Learning - Artificial intelligence or AI is at the forefront of technologies bound to transform the education industry. AI allows content creation, automates grading, generates leads, customizes the learning process, evaluates student performance and aids real-time communication. AI-powered adaptive learning represents a sophisticated type of personalized education. The AI technology enables real-time adaptation of digital learning platforms in line with specific requirements of students to allow delivery of desired exercises and lessons to reinforce concepts and address learning gaps. Continuous improvements in processing power for local devices and the cloud have enabled the use of AI for automated, smart tutoring that is poised to unleash immense opportunities to aid personalized learning. Latest math tutoring tools powered by AI can significantly improve math scores for students in K-8 system. Educational institutions are increasingly betting on AI-based adaptive learning tools for preparing students for competitive exams. In addition, platforms that use machine learning can tailor educational content as per the emotion and learning style of students. While various educational institutions are using the AI technology for grading essays, AI-based chatbots can use adaptive learning for teaching foreign languages.
  • Big Data & Learning Analytics - The exponential surge in digital data volumes is providing numerous opportunities for analytics and deriving actionable insights. Learning analytics and machine learning are anticipated to find a broader role in the field of higher education. The combination of big data and analytics allows teachers and trainers to gain relevant insights and optimize the learning experience. Higher education institutions are leveraging data for tracking student engagement and progress. The approach is enabling instructors to find new ways to improve engagement and provide students with personalized support. The technology is anticipated to find high attention from creators of e-learning courses for tracking engagement to improve learning outcomes. In addition, machine learning, big data and learning analytics are expected to help users in identifying and addressing behavioral or academic challenges faced by students to assist them in harnessing their potential. Educational institutions are increasingly using data related to student learning behavior to draw relevant inferences regarding knowledge of each student. The strategy is paving way for custom learning and enabling students to learn at their own pace.
  • Cloud Tech & IoT - Cloud computing for education or Cloud Tech is predicted to register phenomenal gains, with the technology emerging as an appealing EdTech option for K-12 learning. The technology trend is garnering notable momentum as it extends learning beyond traditional classrooms, streamlines management and helps educational institutions in gaining an edge over competitors. The cloud computing technology holds specific relevance for the K-12 segment due to intriguing factors such as scalability, connectivity and cloud-based ERP systems. Cloud computing is also slated to enable new advances and opportunities for higher education as a result of high scalability, high accessibility, resource availability and intellectual stimulation. Moreover, cloud computing is enforcing collaborations among teachers and students from different parts of the world. The technology also holds a number of other merits like real-time information backup, cost savings, enhanced accountability and better file access. Rising implementation of cloud services is likely to also favor cyber-security trends due to strong focus of educational institutions on a highly secure learning ecosystem. The Internet of Things (IoT) technology is also anticipated to gain notable traction in the education system for connecting devices and components such as lights and whiteboards while supporting tasks such as scientific experiments.
  • Augmented & Virtual Reality - Augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) are trending technologies in the educational environment as they enable immersive and engaging learning experiences for students. Rising affordability of these technologies is anticipated to encourage more schools and higher education institutions to make them an integral component of the curriculum. Increasing popularity of AR and VR in the education system is attributed to their ability to enhance visual learning and turn realistic situations into appealing virtual environments by bringing different types of virtual scenarios and elements. AR allows students to see 3D interpretations of numerous things such as the human body, chemical elements and dinosaurs. The availability of solutions like Adobe Aero has made it convenient for people to create AR objects. On the other hand, VR can be used for interactive virtual laboratories to help STEM students in running experiments in a cost-efficient manner. Advanced tools such as Google Expeditions are enabling virtual field trips by using AR and VR. These technologies eliminate the need for learners to visualize theoretical concepts or watch video demonstrations, and are providing students and educators with opportunities to perform various tasks, including critical surgeries, virtually.
  • Blockchain - The implementation of blockchain is a major EdTech trend with significant potential to disrupt the education system. The technology presents a decentralized, transparent and secure learning ecosystem that allows EdTech platforms to connect teachers, practitioners and learners to desired courses and educational resources. In addition to supporting unique contractual capabilities, the technology enables programming and delivery of lessons and courses. Educational institutions can leverage blockchain to secure records and documents such as admission, payment, grade and attendance. The technology allows learners to collect course certificates and diplomas in a safe manner and have them verified as per the requirement. Blockchain is anticipated to garner more attention in the education industry over the coming years and help learners in enjoying a quality experience. The technology is poised to deliver opportunities such as efficient management of records, remote interactions with tutors through e-learning platform, and affordable learning through MOOCs.
  • E-Learning - Accelerated by the pandemic, e-learning has emerged as a mega trend in the education industry due to its potential to improve retention rates and offer learning opportunities for people across different geographies. E-learning is highly scalable and allows educators or teachers to reach a large number of students at a lower cost in comparison to in-person classes. An increasing number of homeschooling startups are embracing the e-learning model for K-12. In addition, various colleges and universities have started offering online bachelor’s degree programs in the recent years. Several colleges that embraced e-learning amid the pandemic are looking forward to continue with the strategy in the post-pandemic phase. On the other hand, leading platforms such as Coursera are joining hands with universities for offering degree and higher education online. Some of these platforms are also offering various online courses to serve professionals to improve skills. The promising future of e-learning is prompting a large number of startups and service providers to step in for catering to specific needs of different segments.
  • Technology for K-12 Homeschooling - Homeschooling has been witnessing increasing acceptance and interest for the past several years and gained significant impetus during the pandemic that led to closure of a large number of schools across countries. However, standard homeschooling continues relying on conventional approaches, involving pen and paper, and outdated or expensive workbooks. The need is paving way for advanced technology and solutions for enabling tech-powered homeschooling. Latest platforms in this direction offer a blend of live online and in-person learning experiences. These tools are intended to improve outcomes and unlock learner potential. Some of these platforms match teachers and students for online learning while maintaining small groups for personalized delivery. In addition, various players are looking forward to build a full-stack infrastructure to assist parents in homeschooling their children. Advanced tools also help parents in navigating local guidelines and provide parents with educational resources and learning management options. These technological developments are anticipated to offer significant support to the idea of homeschooling and help it in attaining the mainstream status.
  • The Google Clout - Google is enjoying increasing popularity and influence in the education system, and offering a number of compelling tools to serve the domain. The G Suite for Education is a perfect match for Chromebooks that are gaining increasing attention as a device for student learning. The suite comes with various tools such as Google Docs, Forms, Sheets and Gmail and Forms that are widely used by students and educators. G Suite for Education also covers Google Assignments and Google Classroom, which witnessed immense popularity amid the pandemic. While Google Classroom provides educators and students with a digital environment for various aspects such as interactions and assignments, Google Assignments is an advanced tool that assists teachers in creating and grading the coursework in a faster manner. In addition, Google offers Google Cloud Platform that provides universities and schools with cloud storage along with computing power for various applications, including research.
  • Gamification - While gamification is not a new concept in the field of education and has long been used for keeping learners engaged, the game mechanics is anticipated to touch new levels in the digital era. Being a part of edutainment, gamification combines educational games and learning to offer an effective experiential approach for multisensory involvement and knowledge acquisition. The gamification technology holds a number of benefits for education as it improves imagination, creativity and excitement to encourage learning. The approach takes students beyond the paper and pen strategy, and encourages learners for express themselves freely while aiding acquisition of new skills in an innovative, fun way. Gamification for education is poised to receive major impetus from availability of new technologies and tools that are user-friendly in nature, such as Minecraft and Roblox. Minecraft is a sandbox style game that is being used for story writing, creating stage performances and teaching students about DNA. The platform is enjoying high attention for teaching. On the other hand, Roblox is being employed for teaching game design and programming. The education industry is slated to benefit from other platforms. While ClassDojo holds potential for point-based gamification to different subjects, Socrative presents a space race feature to turn quizzes into competitive games. In addition, various institutions are looking forward to implement Knowre for adding gaming mechanics to mathematics. These tools and platforms are anticipated to drive the popularity of gamification for education.
  • IoT - As more educational institutions introduce digital technology as a part of their set-up, they would need more of digitally native infrastructure that could accelerate the use of IoT devices. A case in point is smartphones, which have become integral to the online education sector and a vital means for online learning. Some other potential IoT solutions with promising outlook in the educational technology space include wearables for student monitoring and attendance tracking and smart boards for interactive digital classrooms. There are many institutions who are using IoT for monitoring air quality, monitoring on-campus activity and/or ensuring safety of premises, staff and students.

  • Focus on Cybersecurity Grows Stronger - With big data, analytics and cloud storage in play, the risk of data theft or hacking has increased manifold, necessitating educational institutions upgrade their data security measures. Safeguarding student data, including their names, date of birth, phone numbers and email addresses against cybercrimes is important, and so is to protect students from cyberbullying (bullying through electronic means). Cybersecurity ensures student data is protected with high level of security, and the trend is only expected to gain traction with an increasing number of educational institutions realizing the value of cybersecurity if they are to adopt educational technology. The most used Blockchain features when integrated into e-learning platforms are file verification mechanisms, ID authentication, and tokenized mechanisms for increased affordability.
  • Collaborative & Out of Class Learning Makes Headway - The adoption of educational technology has opened up the possibility of collaborative learning for one and all irrespective of where they are based in the world. Information and communication technology has heralded a new era of e-learning where collaborative features allow students to share and discuss, which further helps them improve their interpersonal skills. Unlike the traditional educational approaches that centered on in-class teaching and learning, the advent and proliferation of mobile digital devices has taken learning beyond the boundaries of a physical classroom. The pandemic has further made e-learning or m-learning popular, and the trend in most likelihood would continue due to the convenience of delivering and receiving education. With complete access to tools including eBooks, bookmarks, search tools, annotation tools, hyperlinks, dictionary and many more, learning becomes interactive and flexible.
  • Social Media based Learning Emerges Into a New Trend - Social media has seeped into the life of every individual with access to internet, including children. With many children aged 10 to 11 years having online social presence on various platforms, many schools and teachers have been proactive in leveraging this trend and turning social media into a powerful tool for augmenting the learning experiences of their students. Educational institutions are increasingly viewing and using social media as a tool to communicate with students where teachers, students and peers can interact, share study materials, projects and opinions. As this platform continues to benefit people of all generations, the trend for social media-based learning is only expected to grow stronger in the coming years.
  • Interactive Classroom Learning Gains in Popularity - Educational technology had first made its way into the classrooms, and since has evolved and grown by leaps and bound. As its classroom journey continues, interactive and lively classrooms continue to benefit millions of students. Interactive classroom learning is primarily based on use of eBooks, which deliver the course material in the form of augmented reality, audio files and videos. An example of the successful implementation of educational technology is the flipped classroom model where students complete learning at home and perform practical work at their school. This form of learning has rendered traditional teaching models obsolete and increased teacher and student interaction over activities, projects, home works, creating a lively interactive environment for learning.
  • Bite Size Learning or Microlearning Becomes the New Flavor of Education - Bite-sized learning/Microlearning provides short but focused learning modules to the target audience whenever and wherever they need it. Modern learners prefer bite-sized learning as it is more mobile friendly. The modules in bite-sized e-learning are small and self-contained. They usually range of 1 - 15 minutes of duration, in contrast to traditional e-learning modules that may last for 30 – 60 minutes. Learners can retain information more easily if it is broken into small chunks and can revisit it whenever required. Microlearning can also be highly beneficial in skill development and offers on-the-job support. Learning in the flow of work refers to learning on-the-job, fast, and with the help of existing tools. However, there have been technical challenges that need to be overcome to improve corporate learning. Almost all the industries have now been turning into APIs so that other can use their services. The use of APIs has been growing in corporate training in microlearning through daily-use tools. With this, learning gets embedded in apps that people use on a daily basis. Learning in the flow of work can be taken to the next level with the following ideas.
  • Art Education Goes Digital - Over the years art students have come to use a number of digital tools for creatively expressing themselves and the trend had gained even more traction amid the pandemic. Prior to the pandemic students used Cinema 4D and Adobe Creative Cloud and such other tools, at their school campuses, for building realistic models of everything from iconic homes to favorite meals. Amid the pandemic students could not access such technology. However, despite that they could make the most of Autodesk Tinkercad and such other browser based programs on their personal Chromebooks for continuing to express their creativity. Arts education is no longer about learning to sculpt, draw or paint. Modern art classes offer students traditional foundation and additionally train them on technologies for creating masterpieces as they might using the regular brushes, pencils or paper. Use of technology by art students was accelerating even before the pandemic. While schools in other areas were forced to transition their regular activities done in classroom to online, in the world of arts, instructors and students were already up to speed with many digital technologies. Technologies give art students much freedom for being creative. Illustration or drawing aps like Procreate, Do Ink or FlipaClip end all the limiting beliefs in students. A few go-to technologies for art teaching and learning are Adobe Creative Cloud, Do Ink, Soundtrap, Adobe Spark, ArtStudio and Seesaw among others. Adobe Creative Cloud is made up of over twenty mobile and desktop apps and includes most of the tools used by professionals.
  • Edutainment to Revolutionize Knowledge Assimilation - Education is opening doors to newer ways of exploring and assimilating the concepts using gamifying and simulated learning environments and integrating artificial intelligence and virtual reality to enhance the experiential learning. The onus of teaching is evolving from thrusting abstract bookish concepts to living the experience through hands on learning and simulation. Using virtual reality and simulation renders learning scenarios authentic and fosters life skills such as critical thinking, team work, time management, situational response and task orientation in students. Artificial Intelligence, augmented and virtual reality are making rapid strides into education sector to enhance the student engagement factor and make learning meaningful by tailoring the content to individual needs using a gamut of specialized tools based on the data. Block chain is being explored to enable closure of learning gaps and promoting better learning outcomes to all students being mindful of their specific requirements. Internet of Things is another emerging technology actively being pursued by educators to enhance focused interaction with parents.
  • Mobile Learning to Become Mainstream - Mobile learning has emerged in K-12 environments and IT leaders are leveraging technology for enhanced flexibility. Device portability has facilitated the new learning environment. Students need devices whether they’re studying from home or class. Charging carts and asset tagging technologies make organizing easier for teachers, students and IT staff. There is increasing awareness amongst educators that there is more to education than conventional classrooms. This may involve working in groups outside of school, the library or even a different part of the school where technology is required to move with the students and a physical environment may not be the best for the skill sets they want to acquire. The pandemic drove home this point and portability became a necessity for learning which will gain more traction in future. Teachers and students are slowly adapting to the digital reality and devices are becoming the new classroom. Device portability is becoming a necessity with the virus variants threatening to emerge. Portable educational technology is here to stay as students below twelve cannot get vaccinated and will have to depend on devices. A robust approach to device portability has many key social and educational benefits. Mobility takes education outside the classroom increasing learning opportunities. Along with robust Internet connections, portability can present immense possibilities.
  • 5G to Emerge as Game Changer for Online Learning - The next-generation (5G) network is expected to make video conferencing more accessible to wider audience. 5G offers the ability to access simultaneous connections and bigger multi-user experience. The network is expected to bring in more subscription services. 5G mobile data networks will remain expensive and operational only in certain areas and major cities till 2021. However, an improvement in coverage and affordability in data plans is anticipated in later years, and businesses and homes are expected to prefer 5G in the future, over wired networks. The second half of 2019 witnessed an increasing number of service providers switch to 5G as more 5G devices became available. Online learning is slated to receive a significant boost from increasing penetration of 4G and 5G technologies in developing and developed countries. However, the evolution to next-generation 5G networks is expected to be somewhat delayed due to the novel virus outbreak. Ultimately, however, there would be pent-up demand from firms for 5G networks in order to gain an edge. 5G smartphones launch from companies would also be delayed.
