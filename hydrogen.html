WHAT'S TRENDING

Hydrogen holds a bright future that is closely tied to green energy transitions and adoption across diverse industries like power generation and transport. Hydrogen presents an appealing opportunity to the world for tap it as an integral component of clean, affordable and secure energy future. Clean hydrogen is witnessing increasing business and political attention, which is reflected by the phenomenal growth in number of projects and policy decisions. The time is now to scale up associated technologies and push down costs for enabling widespread adoption of hydrogen. In the coming years, hydrogen is anticipated to emerge as a key element of new strategies for dealing with energy challenges due to its potential to de-carbonize industries such as chemicals, long-haul transport and iron/steel. Hydrogen is set to garner attention amid rising energy-related carbon emissions and intensifying climate goals as it can ensure robust energy security and improve the air quality. Companies are anticipated to explore innovative methods to produce, transport, store, and use hydrogen in various ways. The hydrogen economy is expected to gain from ongoing projects to use different types of fuels for production of clean hydrogen. The fuel is witnessing encouraging projects related to infrastructure development and methane or electricity generation for powering households and feeding industries while supporting the transport sector.

Hydrogen offers a promising route to de-carbonization and can help the world in mitigating climate change attributed to unabated carbon emissions. Hydrogen can match with any fuel while dramatically reducing the climate impact. Hydrogen can be exploited for powering vehicles and energy generation without carbon emissions. Clean hydrogen is slated to receive a significant lift and impetus from confluence of a number of factors. Sweeping legislation in the US in recent years is creating new avenues to bring down costs of low-carbon hydrogen while catalyzing demand. The lingering fossil fuel crisis in Europe owing to the Russia-Ukraine war is prompting governments to explore alternatives to Russian natural gas. The region is witnessing serious efforts to reduce hydrogen cost and make it competitive to other options. Europe has come up with a hydrogen-hub strategy that entails significant funding for numerous projects focusing on aspects like new technology through to industrial adoption. The move is paving way for construction of various hubs in the region, with Rotterdam emerging as a prominent hydrogen port. These endeavors are poised to support hydrogen over the coming years and help it in attaining the status of the conventional energy. Hydrogen is bound to gather more steam ahead with rising costs and rising concerns over future availability of fossil fuels. The involvement of various aspects, including production, transportation, storage and use is bound to build a comprehensive value chain for hydrogen, paving for a vibrant hydrogen economy

Hydrogen holds potential to emerge as a compelling option for powering the clean economy of the future. The fuel is bound to gain from ongoing technological advancements intended to allow cost-efficient production through renewable energy, ensure underground storage for extended periods and enable on-demand supply to power different applications, from steel manufacturing units through to ships. With policy and regulatory support to favor scaling up of the technology, hydrogen production is anticipated to become an affordable option. The production cost of clean hydrogen is poised to decline significantly in the coming years, enabling the fuel to compete with natural gas in various countries. Hydrogen is likely to emerge as an attractive fuel, even after including the cost associated with the pipeline infrastructure and storage. Global expansion of the hydrogen economy is poised to allow efficient delivery of clean gas to industrial customers. Efforts to scale up clean hydrogen production are expected to help challenging sectors in swiftly moving towards de-carbonization. The likely decline in prices is slated to expedite the transition from coal and other fossil fuels towards clean hydrogen. The fuel is anticipated to find acceptance in the steel manufacturing industry, cement production units for heat, production of various chemicals and the shipping domain. In the coming years, hydrogen is likely to emerge as a replacement for diesel for heavy-duty vehicles such as trucks. However, broader acceptance of the fuel and these uses of hydrogen for de-carbonization goals rely heavily on policy support and advancements related to production of clean hydrogen as well as safe distribution. While hydrogen can be used as an alternative to oil, gas and coal in different applications, tapping environmental benefits associated with the fuel requires stakeholders to shift attention from fossil fuels towards clean sources for hydrogen production. Existing approaches for clean generation of hydrogen are expensive in nature. In addition, there are concerns associated with safe storage and transport of hydrogen. While clean hydrogen represents a small industry and marked by high costs, the scenario is changing rapidly with continuing efforts towards infrastructure development to help hydrogen in attaining same energy security as enjoyed by natural gas.


  • Being a reliable and cost-efficient option for generating electricity, hydrogen is garnering attention in various mature markets such as Europe and North America due to their focus on clean energy generation. Hydrogen production is anticipated to receive a major stimulus from initiatives to push renewable energy, expansion of power generation and cement, and rising supply related to heavy oils. Ongoing developments are also addressing safety issues associated with hydrogen distribution as it represents a highly flammable fuel. In the recent years, various countries have started focusing on new pipelines and retrofitting of existing natural gas pipelines for regional distribution. Natural gas pipeline networks are anticipated to play a vital role in distribution of hydrogen in the US, Canada and Russia. While ships and trains are being used to transport the fuel in gaseous or liquid form, liquid tankers and tube trainers are prudent options to distribute hydrogen via highways. Increasing proliferation of hydrogen refueling stations is anticipated to favor availability of the fuel along highways, mitigating issues faced by hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, mainly buses and trucks. Moreover, various startups are offering transportation services for hydrogen supply along with advanced solutions for compressed hydrogen gas storage. The hydrogen economy is slated to receive support from ongoing efforts by various players to build a robust hydrogen refueling network, which is primarily intended to serve heavy vehicles. These refueling networks cover centralized production plants with distributed generation, third-party generation and distributed refueling.
  • The hydrogen economy is witnessing concrete efforts to push production and distribution using advanced technologies and diverse means of hydrogen delivery. Led by China, the Asia-Pacific region accounts for the commanding stake on the hydrogen market due to the presence of various refineries across countries like China and India. Hydrogen production in the region is expected to be further favored by ongoing efforts by countries such as Australia and Japan to investigate sustainable technologies for production. Hydrogen producers from Asia-Pacific are focusing on South Africa, Indonesia and Vietnam to drive business. In addition, various US players are making efforts to expand operations across these nations to grow hydrogen production.
  • Despite holding notable de-carbonization potential, hydrogen needs active efforts to ensure clean hydrogen generation and broader uptake. While hydrogen represents a clean-burning option and an alternative to conventional fuels for various applications, majority of the existing approaches to produce hydrogen rely on fossil fuels that lead to unsustainable hydrogen generation. Existing methods to split water into oxygen and hydrogen for obtaining renewable hydrogen involve the use of electricity produced using fossil fuels. Despite notable decline in costs over the past few years, the electrolyzer technology for the process remains expensive for scaling. Efforts such as carbon capture and storage can enable clean hydrogen. However, these approaches are costly and fail to help hydrogen in gaining a competitive edge over traditional fuels. Moreover, hydrogen storage and distribution hold their own set of challenges and require massive investments in infrastructure development and upgrades.

How things would play out in 2023 and beyond will be largely driven by certain key trends in the area of corporate diversity, in addition to the actions that leaders would take around these trends.


  • Managing the Evolving Remote Workforce - Even as the pandemic recedes to the endemic stage and businesses are resuming operations with office space open as usual, the remote work model is continuing to co-exist with the in-office set-up. With a certain number employees in the workforce still working remotely, businesses face an uphill task. Considering each of the WFH employee works under a different living condition, the challenges are unique with no single solution. As in case of women employees, they need to balance work and home, and might lack a dedicated space for work from where they can focus on work alone. Businesses need to brainstorm means to bridge such gaps and overcome challenges of this distributed model.
  • Managing the Multigenerational Workforce - The current businesses work with a workforce that is the most diverse of all times. The characteristic feature of this workforce is the co-existence of employees that belong to five different generations. In companies working with all these five generations of people, a typical workforce mix would comprise the silent generation (people born between 1928 and 1945), the baby boomers (born between 1946 and 1964), the generation X (born between 1965 and 1980), the generation Y or millennials (born between 1981 and 96) and the newest of all generation Z (from 1997 and continuing). Individually, people from each of these generations have unique expectations from their work lives, and bring with them different voices, skills and life experiences to their workplace that ultimately shapes a companyâ€™s work culture. Such a diverse workforce spanning five generations requires employers, business leaders and human resource managers to be well acquainted with the capabilities and characteristics of each of the generation, and have well framed strategies to make employees of all generations feel inclusive and celebrated for who they are.
  • Purging Unconscious Bias at Workplaces - Unconscious bias, the tendency to form opinions, process information and arrive at decisions on the basis of inherent or unconscious prejudices and biases towards certain groups of people or individuals, impacts the type of relationship an employer or business leader develops with each of its employee. Since this relationship influences the decisions that are made at workplaces, where a truly inclusive workforce comprises of people with varied ethnicity, backgrounds and experiences, it is important that businesses and their leaders have strategies in place that ensure such biases and prejudices are purged or controlled regularly.
  • Embracing Diverse Gender Identity & Expression - The recent years have seen awareness increase about the diverse gender identities and various gender expressions. As organizations the world have on their payroll people with such diverse gender identities, they need to adopt inclusive practices that may include providing gender-neutral restrooms, employee health benefits that support transitioning individuals, and creating a mindful atmosphere where use of inclusive language is encouraged to communicate with transgender or non-binary employees, among others.
  • Preparing for Systemic Change - Over the previous couple of years, there has been a rise in instances of systemic injustices reflective of the flaws that exist globally on institutional level. Appalling crimes against innocent people of color have brought to fore the unjust and racist nature of society that continues to exists even in the most developed nations. Businesses need to incorporate some critical systemic changes that would positively influence both the workplaces and societies at large. They can ensure racial discrimination is kept at bay by introducing policies that stress on safety of people of all races and create positive experiences at workplaces
  • Improved Transparency in Goal - A business with a diverse workforce need not imply a higher level of inclusion and equity. Even when organizations step up efforts to increase their diversity, the same might not translate into greater efforts at inclusivity. In order to become a truly diverse and inclusive organization, businesses would need to set transparent goals and targets and introduce relevant D&I initiatives. These measures would make those leading the organization more accountable, promote honest interactions between employees and their superiors, and foster an environment where employees feel encouraged to share their viewpoint, solutions and/or ideas.
  • The Time to Hire Diversity Professionals Has Come - With workplaces becoming more diverse, companies might require professional help. Diversity professionals are one of the newest categories of experts that organizations are hiring to address their D&I requirements. Diversity professionals come with the vision and mission to create a diverse and inclusive workplace, and handle multiple roles involving human resource and recruitment, as well as leadership responsibilities. They might need to multitask to organize and conduct D&I related educational sessions, introduce novel ideas for improved D&I atmosphere at a workplace, create an inclusive workplace culture, encourage all to use inclusive language, manage workplace discrimination or harassment related complaints, among others. The need for and role of diversity professionals is only expected to grow in the coming years.
  • Supporting Mental Health of Employees - Supporting mental health of employees has emerged as a priority for most organizations in order to ensure that their diverse talent pool continues to perform efficiently for optimal operational performance of a business. Businesses are aware of the toll the global pandemic and its repercussions have taken on the mental wellbeing of employees, and the difficulties their employees are facing in adjusting to the new normal. While some lost their jobs as businesses closed down, others had to adjust to the new work-from-home model, all while living in the fear of contracting coronavirus, which created a stressful environment for most people. By creating a supportive environment, businesses can ensure that metal health of their employees does not get compromised. They may achieve this through simpler means such as by encouraging managers, supervisors and peers to converse and empathize with each other.
  • Avoiding Gestures of Tokenism - Prioritizing diversity and inclusion means much more that simply hiring a woman in a boardroom or team full of men, or making way for one people of color in the workplace. Such a move could simply reflect an attitude of tokenism. Organizations need to be mindful in their approach towards D&I, and incorporate strategies and policies that truly promote diversity and inclusion.
