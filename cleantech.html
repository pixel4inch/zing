WHAT'S TRENDING

Cleantech is moving towards large scale application and mainstream adoption. Smart technologies and software are eliminating the many previous issues concerning reliability and cost. Cleantech is seen as holding potential for major market disruption and also attractive for investors. Companies, especially startups with the most potentially disruptive innovations, are being sought by investors. Investors are placing their money in these innovations not just to speed up tech adoption cycle but for their own benefit because they offer considerable growth opportunities. The pandemic had also brought to fore the capacity of the world to adapt and adapt quickly to change and also innovate as needed. The drastically changed circumstances changed the way the world operated. Technology is being used immensely in daily lives. Even in the post-pandemic era, the human society would continue to welcome change with the same vigor and be similarly willing to adapt. This positive change in the thinking would drive more businesses, governments and also consumers to embrace clean innovations and technologies earlier in their lifecycle.


Clean technologies are touted as helping the world meet the SDG7 that call for providing access to reliable, affordable, modern and sustainable energy for all. Cleantech can help nations move towards realizing SDG7 by accelerating off-grid solar energy uptake and deployment of cleaner cooking fuels and other clean solutions. Investments in renewable energy, especially in developing markets, have been increasing continuously, despite the many challenges. Mexico, Argentina, Chile, Peru and Costa Rica in Latin America for instance, have been witnessing increased investments in renewable energy projects. Furthermore, renewable energy is just one aspect of the cleantech movement helping the world move towards a resource-efficient, carbon-neutral and digitized future. There are several other technologies employing which, the world seeks to achieve energy access, water and air quality and long-term climate goals. Sustainable technologies for the agriculture, logistics, and transportation sectors and innovations for industrial biotechnology and manufacturing processes would also be critical aspects of future cleantech. Cleantech includes all those technologies currently being researched, for development, for realizing the goal of Net-Zero civilization, in which GHG emissions release into the atmosphere is limited to the levels of what is possible to remove or absorb. This definition of cleantech indicates that the field would be rife with opportunities for investment, entrepreneurship and also careers, in the years ahead.


Cleantech firms are getting their competitive advantage form smart software which is allowing the companies to operate and manage their assets, remotely. Batteries and solar panels for example, can be managed remotely, and more efficiently with the help of software. This ultimately enhances the technology’s reliability, increases sustainability and reduces cost. High costs and low reliability have so far been the major concerns contributing towards hesitancy of renewable energy, one of the cleantechs. Smart technology holds the potential to eliminate such concerns and grant the necessary competitive edge and such advantages create opportunities for investors. The energy sector would thus be disrupted by cleantech. And, when power is provided at a lower cost and in a reliable manner, there would be increased chances of people shifting away from conventional methods and embracing clean technologies. This way, cleantech’s disruptive potential would result in at-scale adoption of technologies going forward. Government policy will also help accelerate cleantech adoption. In the U.S., the new climate plan would help speed up renewable power and other clean technology growth in the years ahead. The infrastructure deal of President Biden is one ambitious deal worth US$73 billion for upgrading the nation’s electricity grid. Improved access for consumers, to clean energy, is one of the major aspects of the deal. Such deals, inspire confidence for investors in cleantech. The US plans to put in US$2 trillion over the coming decades, in technologies of clean energy, for achieving its goal of decarbonization. Several other countries worldwide, are similarly pushing towards achieving net-zero emission targets, and would take the help of cleantech. The market for cleantech would benefit considerably from this. Goals for reaching net-zero emissions are in place for close to 130 countries presently and these goals would drive government policies which would in turn encourage companies and consumers alike to adopt cleantech early on in their lifecycles helping it to move speedily towards mainstream use. There is therefore immense market potential for clean technology.

  • Rise of Net Zero Energy Buildings (NZEB) – Construction of NZEBs holds tremendous potential in in overcoming climate change and global warming. NZEBs are typically constructed with significant energy saving features. The cooling and heating loads are lowered through the use of highly-efficient equipment, such as heat pumps, additional insulation particularly in the basement and the attic, installation of high-efficiency appliances and LED lighting, and highly-efficient windows, such as triple-glazed, low-emissivity windows, natural ventilation, passive shading during the summer and passive solar heat gain during winter, as well as other techniques. Loads for heating water can be reduced through the use of solar heating, heat recovery units on wastewater, water conservation fixtures, and highly-efficient water heating equipment. Moreover, daylighting with solartubes and skylights can facilitate in providing complete daytime illumination within the house. Nighttime illumination is generally achieved with LED and fluorescent lighting that utilize significantly lower amounts of power as compared to incandescent lights, and that too without the addition of any unwanted heat. Efficiency gains made possible by these technologies would allow the global buildings sector to reduce its CO2 emissions by 350 Mt, by 2050. NZE buildings also necessitates occupant behavioral changes such as changing the temperature setting for space heating and reducing use of hot water that is excessively heated, among others. Such changes are expected to lead to reduction of around 250 Mt CO2 by 2030. Increased utilization of low temperature water for washing clothes and using line drying of clothes and such other changes also facilitate energy efficiency and Decarbonization. Share of fossil fuels used by NZE buildings is projected to come down to 30% by 2030 and to 2% by 2050.
  • Living Walls & Green Roofs -Green rooftops and vertical gardens are poised to break up the monotony related to traditional buildings and create a future full of green structures. These green ideas are taking shape and enjoying increasing acceptance due to the pressing need to adapt urban centers to unfolding risks attributed to climate change and extreme weather events that hold devastating consequences for the economy, human health & wellbeing and the essential infrastructure. Living walls and green roofs are capable of alleviating the impact of climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide, improving the air quality and regulating temperatures. Living green walls and green roofs are a unique and modern approach that incorporates nature into buildings and spaces. Living walls can be effectively installed on different indoor and outdoor structures for environmental benefits and appealing aesthetics. Living walls with plants are capable of playing an important role in carbon sequestering. In addition, vertical gardens hold potential to improve the biological diversity by supporting mini ecosystems. Living walls support various beneficial organisms like bees, butterflies, humming birds and ladybugs. Living green walls and green roofs improve the air quality, reduce energy consumption, act as acoustic filter and improve health and wellbeing of occupants. These merits are poised to benefit living walls and rooftops forests, and help them in becoming a critical part of future eco-friendly green building designs in the coming years.
  • Responsible Construction With Green Building Materials -Green materials are increasingly integrated into construction practices, heralding a path for greener life. Recyclable steel and reclaimed wood forms a major part of the green market. Currently, there is a concrete effort to convert materials, for example concrete to green staples. In addition to non-toxicity, bio-degradability and recyclability, maintenance and sturdiness are also key considerations. Use of green building materials is increasing in both retrofitting existing buildings and new construction projects. One such sustainable material include Smart Bricks, which are clay hollow blocks that are resource-efficient. Installation of roof-top solar panels is also on the increase. The most used material in building construction is concrete. Cement production generates close to 10% of the worldwide carbon emissions. Researchers and scientists therefore, have been trying to produce more eco-friendly concrete and self-healing concrete has been the first step in that direction. This self-healing concrete has live bacteria which remain dormant until cracks in concrete are exposed to air and moisture. Once exposed to the elements, the bacterial spores germinate and eat up the calcium lactate in cement and produce limestone. The limestone then fills the crevices and cracks and thus allows the concrete to heal on its own. This new concrete thus prevents costly maintenance job for filling up cracks and crevices and therefore can be among the sustainable building materials. A few other sustainable building/construction products include green roofs; cooling roofs; cement alternatives; recycled steel; natural insulation (foam, hemp and wool insulation); eco-friendly flooring (recycled materials and bamboo among others); VOC-free paints; air purifying paints; cabinets and countertops made of salvaged and recycled materials; efficient lighting fixtures made of recycled materials and xeriscaping (using plants that require very less water) among others.
  • Carbon Capture and Storage Will Hit the Spotlight - CCS is a critical tool for mitigating climate change and the pipeline of CSS projects has grown considerably in the recent years. As of 2021, there were 44 nations and the EU that disclosed their targets of net-zero-emissions. There is also this climate ambition alliance bringing together regions, nations, cities and also investors for achieving net-zero emissions, by 2050. The alliance has over 2300 companies that have pledged to achieve these emissions goals. There are presently more than 130 CCS facilities worldwide. However, a network of carbon capture necessitates an expansive geological storage and identifying a location that would be ideal for storage would also require huge initial investment. Recently however, a trend developed in which projects started sharing CO2 transport including port facilities, pipelines, storage wells and shipping, for reducing these costs. There are also several new technologies deployed for removing carbon altogether. One such technology is blue hydrogen. Blue hydrogen refers to the process where in fossil fuels are deployed for producing clean hydrogen. There are currently, several blue hydrogen projects ongoing, providing cleaner hydrogen fuel sources for helping decarbonize. Zero emission energy targets can also be achieved with WtE (Waste-to-Energy) plants. Furthermore, since CCS projects would be complex there is this need for strategic partnerships among different organizations. A large network of related organizations can streamline projects development. Projects would also be able to better stand against setbacks when they are well-funded. Heavy industries for instance, are rapidly embracing the technology as a more realistic pathway towards decarbonization. In 2021 alone, there were more than a hundred new facilities that were announced. A major cement producer called HeidelbergCement for instance, is developing around eight initiatives for carbon capture, worldwide, while the largest oil company, Aramco, had also been developing a large facility for carbon capture for hydrogen production. The large steel manufacturer, ArcelorMittal, also considerably depends on carbon capture. All these companies have committed themselves to achieving net zero emissions, by 2050. The companies are gradually moving from the pilot projects capable of capturing just 400 tons of carbon/day to projects that can capture more than 13 thousand
  • Renewable Energy Storage to Witness Technology Progress - Several companies worldwide are developing innovative long term storage for power generated from renewable energy sources. A company called Vionx Energy, developed a vanadium-based redox flow battery. In flow batteries, energy is stored in the form of electrolyte and not as electrode material. The electrolyte gets dispensed from tank and is recovered in the loop, for re-energization. Longer life, quick recharge, no emissions, faster response time and a flexible layout are a few benefits with flow batteries. When compared to lithium ion batteries, flow batteries have higher current and power density. In addition to battery chemistries for maintaining higher capacity and longer life, energy storage also needs more of power electronics. Using sensors, advanced IoT analyzing techniques and two way communication, the storage, charging as well as release of the energy can happen in a controlled and smart fashion. There would also be several companies in the future, that would offer ESaaS (Energy Storage-as-a-Service). For the world, energy storage helps reduce climate change through decreasing emissions from heating & cooling activities while at the level of a community, it would enable better integration of energy that is produced locally. For individual users, energy storage offers improved control over energy costs. For the private sector, ESS opens up newer business opportunities. The private sector can offer ESaaS and other services like installation of the storage systems, virtual power plants and charging stations among others. ESaaS in particular is likely to emerge as the business concept with the most promise over the coming years. In an ESaaS model, energy would be made available to customers in the form of a service similar to how food, films, music etc, is offered on subscriptions.
  • Carbon Tracking and Reporting Will Become a Must Have Carbon Footprint Management Tool – Carbon tracking refers to tracking and gauging carbon emissions from various sources, both indirect and direct. It enables organizations to calculate GHG emissions from their various business operations and understand their footprint and also the ways to reduce it. Carbon reporting happens after tracking and is relates to publishing of the data that has been gathered. It aids in creating standards for reporting and helps establish transparency and facilitates comparisons among enterprises. Reporting happens on a regular basis and enables in understanding an organization’s progress with regards to its efforts related to environment sustainability. Proper emissions reports will offer information related to overall emissions and also the activities and areas of a business contributing to them. They offer analysis and insights. They also aid in creating novel goals or modifying existing goals on the basis of the insights. Further, they indicate opportunities for reducing emissions. Carbon accounting converts data related to carbon emissions into numeric data, thereby quantifying the emissions. This aids in setting practical targets and helps understands the initiatives that need to be taken to attain them. Most governments are requiring some big companies to track and report their CO2 emissions. The EU needs large-scale companies to publish reports on the impact of their environmental and social activities on a regular basis. A good GHG accounting system should correctly measure the carbon impacts of the supply chain of every company and offer insights to make the right decisions. Reporting related to the progress made related to net zero targets.
  • Vertical Farming Will Receive New Impetus - Vertical farming can aid in addressing the deforestation issue. The method is also reliable and facilitates crop production all through the year. In vertical farming, the requirement of water is reduced by 95% or more as aquaponic and hydroponic systems are used. Also, dehumidifiers are used, which gather, recycle, and reuse the water produced by plants during transpiration. It also eliminates the transportation of produce over long distances, as vertical farms are generally established close to urban areas in abandoned locations. This enables the delivery of food to customers in a short period in a fresher state, which lowers food spoilage and wastage and helps reduce the transportation emissions and costs involved. Further, in this farming approach, the problem of pests is non-existent, as plants are cultivated in a closed and controlled space. This eliminates the need for pesticide and herbicide usage. The need to use conservation agents is also not present, as the produce is transported over short distances. The method also prevents eutrophication. And while vertical farms need energy for providing artificial lighting and water and nutrients for the plants, most of the cost and negative environmental impact can be offset using approaches such as thermal insulation and renewable energy sources. Further, in vertical farms the risk of contamination of irrigation water is low, which reduces the likelihood of disease outbreaks.

They do not produce harmful emissions. Hydrogen by 2050, would power more than 400 million cars and up to 20 million buses. The plentiful and versatile hydrogen could offer great help to nations in meeting their climate targets. It is already a component of most of the EC’s net zero emission scenarios, for 2050 while in the US, there have been several DoE initiatives spurring considerable investments in hydrogen economy. The capability of sustainably producing and transporting hydrogen could turn it into a daily alternative energy source the world urgently needs. Investments in development of hydrogen solutions are also increasingly considerably. Hydrogen would also play a key role in securing energy supplies for the future and this was highlighted recently with the Russia-Ukraine war. European nations are heavily dependent on Russian energy and the war hastened many countries in the continent to seek means for reducing this dependence. The EC released a plan for phasing out reliance on fossil fuels from Russia and using renewable hydrogen instead. The proposal is for quadrupling the present targets for hydrogen supplies, by 2030. Another encouraging factor for blue and green hydrogen has been the rise of ESG initiatives and customers becoming keen on making the planet sustainable. From tech giants and major retailers to leaders in the transportation sector, there are currently several organizations pledging to cut down emissions in their supply chains and embracing hydrogen for them makes perfect economic sense.
  • Circular Waste Management Will Gain in Prominence – The world is generating around 2 billion tons of solid municipal waste each year and a third of it is not managed in a manner that is environmentally safer. The factors of population growth, rapid urbanization and haphazard development would result in a 70 percent in global waste over the coming three decades. The East APAC is currently the region generating the highest amount of waste in the world, a quarter of it almost. High income countries, even though account for just 15% of the global population, are also generating significant amounts of solid waste. Generation of waste increases with increase in economic development. Converting waste into usable products and materials, referred to as upcycling, is another popular trend currently. Companies across the world are developing and using innovative means for upcycling waste into everything from fertilizers and fuel to bicycles and clothes. Similar to several other industries, waste management must also proceed towards becoming digitized and data-driven to make operations more efficient. The data can be used for identifying fill patterns and optimizing driver schedules and routes for reducing operational costs. The cost of sensors needed for collecting such data is also constantly decreasing. Smart collection bins would also decrease the amount of manual labor needed. The time spent on waste management is reduced. Smart methods would also result in improved sanitation and hygiene, and ultimately mitigate disease vectors which breed in the open waste. Public health can thus be improved and thereby quality of life enhanced. Significant amount of recycling would also be witnessed in waste management in the future. Waste would be converted into energy. The future of waste management would also include IoT-enabled practices, improved monitoring systems and several other technology-based advancements.
  • Steel Industry to Witness Major Decarbonizing Efforts - One major step forward for the global construction industry, towards becoming green, would be through decarbonizing steel. Partial decarbonization of steel can be achieved by encouraging use of the new furnace technologies that are natural gas-powered. They can also use the more energy efficient direct reduced iron and HBI (Hot Briquetted Iron) for making steel which lead to lesser GHG emissions than the conventional BOFs (Basic Oxygen Furnaces) or coke-fueled blast furnaces. BOFs continue to account for more than 75% of the production of steel globally and are predominant in China and Europe. Steel CO2 can also be lowered through retrofitting BOFs for CCS (Carbon Capture & Storage) and CCU (Carbon Capture & Utilization). These would sequester around 85% of carbon emissions from the coke feedstock. A switch can also be made to furnaces that are hydrogen-powered but the issue here would be the availability of such low-carbon hydrogen. A cleaner, blue hydrogen involves combining steam and fossil fuels for producing carbon dioxide and hydrogen and storing that carbon dioxide underground. An even cleaner method would be to employ electrolysis for separating water and hydrogen for procuring green hydrogen. Furthermore, for the construction sector striving to move towards greener steel, there are significant structural and commercial barriers in addition to the aforementioned technical challenges. The low margins of the sector continue to be the major limiting factor. This reduces producer interest in green technologies. Conventional approaches are able to produce the metal for around US$500/ metric ton, at slab level, whereas the near-zero emission steel production is way more expensive. Structural steel can also be replaced with CLT (Cross-Laminated Timber) and glulam beams. Such methods would also help in reducing embodied carbon.

  • Energy Usage Management Will Become a Key Focus Area - For lowering energy wastage, management and optimization of energy usage is being concentrated upon. Data centers are among the high energy consumers. However, infrastructural developments are reducing their energy requirements. Liquid cooling and modular data centers are some of such solutions, with the former aiding in preventing overheating of machines. In a similar manner, energy monitoring systems regulate the environment indoors automatically to reduce energy load. Big data and AI are also being used for the improvement of these systems. Jetcool is a start-up in the US that offers direct-to-chip liquid cooling technology for application in data centers. The microconvective technology reduces energy consumption by optimizing compute and reducing PUE (power usage effectiveness). The technology is driven by thermal resistance systems (ultra-low). Further, the solutions of the company lower water usage and energy expenses through the replacement of high water and power-consuming systems. By comprising processors such as ASICs, GPUs, and CPUs, the technology helps in lowering energy usage in big data centers.
