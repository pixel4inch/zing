WHAT'S TRENDING

Music has long been the mainstream mode of entertainment. The music industry is evolving continuously. The music industry is a dominant sector that constantly experiments with developments in technology to make highly sophisticated music and augment the listeners' experience. Numerous developments have been observed in the music industry transforming the way music is produced, disseminated, and consumed. A more significant cataclysm is anticipated in the market in case blockchain technologies are implemented simultaneously with the extensive availability of music NFTs and Web 3.0 in the near future. Technology is changing the way music is created, composed, produced and mostly importantly delivered to people. From music cassettes to CDs/DVDs to downloads on pen drives to live streaming of personalized music libraries, technology continues to change the way music is consumed worldwide. Cloud music in this regard is the newest delivery platform for music in the entertainment industry. Gradual development of the ecosystem supporting the cloud has been playing a pivotal role in the commercialization of the cloud platform in the music industry. The development of cloud music storage services, music lockers and high resolution and audio quality cloud-based music streaming services, rapid penetration of smartphones, tablets and other mobile computing devices, ubiquitous availability of affordable mobile Internet connectivity for instance have catalyzed the commercialization of cloud music. The e-Commerce industry is migrating into the same day delivery of products and services. The media, entertainment and broadcasting industry is also following suit with on-demand services like Video-On-Demand (VOD), cloud gaming, and cloud music. Music streaming is emerging as a perfect option for mobile music than download stores. Streaming is forecast to account for the lion’s share of the mobile music market. Major streaming service giants such as Spotify, YouTube, Apple Music and Google Play Music are revolutionizing this business model. Moreover, higher penetration of smartphones and device/tariff bundling along with affordable data plans such as unlimited and zero-rated data plans are encouraging users to enjoy music without concerns about cost or data connectivity. The growing popularity of wearable devices against the backdrop of the rise of consumer grade Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem and the resulting diversification of smartphone vendors into manufacturing smartwatches to provide users with a connected experience is bringing in disruptive changes in the music industry. Mobile music streaming service providers are increasingly focusing on the development of interfaces and applications compatible with wearable devices, allowing music streaming on such device without the need for smartphones to access music. Subscription-based music streaming continues to rise in popularity backed by growing consumer willingness to pay for special services and premium content.

Online video has emerged as the newest entertainment medium for modern population. Consumption of online video grew dramatically over the past few years due to availability of quality and engaging video content on highly successful video sites such as YouTube, Hulu, Vimeo and Netflix among others. Currently, a significant portion of video consumption is emanating from smartphones and tablet PCs, which offer on-to-the-go video viewing at the convenience of user’s palm. While YouTube, Netflix, Facebook and similar video sharing and social networking sites remain popular in conventional PC and laptop video consumption, smartphones have included additional video platforms such as messaging apps, thereby enhancing overall video consumption. Through messaging apps such as Whatsapp, Snapchat, WeChat, Kakaotalk and Line, millions of video clips are being shared every day, thus expanding the total video consumption on a regular basis. Going forward, majority TV content would also be adapted to match preferences of expanding online audiences.

Global video games market continues to grow at a healthy rate, surpassing revenues of music industry and box office revenues. Video gaming revenues are dependent completely on consumer spending levels, although the business model has change considerably in the recent times. Unlike earlier times, present day consumers tend to purchase fewer games but spend greater time on these games. Consequently, the business model is shifting towards recurring type of revenues being generated by active user base, which contrasts to the earlier model that relied on sales of single units. Due to this changing business model, the focus on video game industry players is on increasing the per user engagement rates. For this, companies are looking to develop highly engaging games and also looking to adopt innovative in-game monetization opportunities. The integration of additional downloadable content such as expansion packs, characters, tools, and new features present gaming companies with added avenues to generate revenues. Such a business model has evolved in line with improvements being witnessed in Internet connectivity, especially mobile Internet services, and advancements in gaming hardware, which are enabling users to gain access to a wide range of high-quality games across varied platforms and devices. Mobile gaming in fact currently accounts for a growing share of revenue generation for the video gaming industry.


The immense popularity of certain video games is expected to help them influence cultural discourse, leading to live concerts, new albums and new movies. The tremendous popularity of various games along with their playtime and number of active users is hard for other participants to ignore. In the coming years, players across different segments of the entertainment industry are anticipated to engage in partnerships, with media companies eying on the opportunity presented by surge in gaming. These developments are expected to also create new growth opportunities for associated industries. On the other hand, the COVID-19 pandemic is anticipated to legitimatize and popularize eSports, which is attributed to adoption across broadcasters, athletes and leagues looking forward to engage fans. Extended shutdown for conventional sports leagues is expected to drive young users towards eSports, providing the segment with a substantial customer base. Recent developments also indicate that eSports is likely to emerge as mainstream option in the industry.


Other major factors promoting growth of video games are technological advancements in software and hardware, increased internet access, increased smartphone penetration, and easy access to games. Developers of video games are continuously pushing their limits in the area of graphics for making the games more gripping, which is anticipated to further promote market growth. Industry participants are currently more focused on increasing hardware efficiency and compatibility due to the increased inclination toward online games. MMO (Massively Multiplayer Online), F2P (Free2Play) and other multiplayer games gained much popularity in the recent years and the trend would to continue in the same manner for many more years to come. Consumers are also spending excessively on video games because of increasing disposable incomes. Increasing popularity of social media gaming would also positively impact market growth over the coming years. Number of people using social networking sites like Reddit and Facebook for gaming had increased tremendously in the recent years. Increased availability of games in different genres including role play, action, strategy and simulation, on these social networking sites, has been attracting players. There are also now eSports tournaments being organized. Expanding professional gamer numbers would also lead to increase in video games as well as accessories sales going forward.

  • Cloud Music Revolutionizes the Music Industry - Cloud music is the newest delivery platform for music in the entertainment industry. Cloud Music allows users to access digital music files over the cloud at leisure as ‘on-demand streaming services’ over their Inter-enabled devices such as smartphones, tablets, iPads and PCs. The cloud music is a step ahead of digital download services, as it takes music portability and convenience to the next level by allowing users to stream music anywhere and at anytime without having to worry about downloading music onto their devices. Cloud music services provide users with the power of on-demand playback facility, which allows them to choose and stream their favorite music on their mobile handsets rather than listen to what can be referred to as radio services. Cloud music has also taken personalization of music to another level, enhancing both portability and customizability for music listeners while handling their music files over the Internet. Cloud music streaming technique is ideal for situations where music delivery is needed instantaneously, including live broadcasts and when sampling of music files prior to their purchase is required. Cloud music over a computer or other media devices requires essentials such as Internet connection along with hardware such as sound card and speakers or headphones. In cloud music streaming, a mere click on the music title on a website results in its play instantaneously, either on the website itself or in a pop-up window.

  • Growing Popularity of Wearable Devices - The growing popularity of wearable devices against the backdrop of the rise of consumer grade Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem and the resulting diversification of smartphone vendors into manufacturing smartwatches to provide users with a connected experience, is bringing in disruptive changes in the music industry. There is the growing trend of integrating cloud music streaming services in wearable devices such as smartwatches. Mobile music streaming service providers are increasingly focusing on the development of interfaces and applications compatible with wearable devices, allowing music streaming on such device without the need for smartphones to access music. For example, Apple developed Apple Watch that displays time, helps users to access maps, track health, access Wi-Fi and Bluetooth, and play music by syncing with their iPhone. Spotify launched Spotify Running service in the year 2015, which picks upbeat songs at the same tempo as the runner’s step rates, ranging from 140-190 steps/min, using sensors on a smartphone. The running playlist is chosen based on the listening history and stride rate of users. In addition, Rock My World, Inc. launched Rockmyrun app that uses step rate as well as heart rate stats collected from smartwatch to select a playlist from different genres, which is played during various exercises. Google also recently voiced plans to launch music streaming services as part of their wearable devices. A wireless communication protocol is used for communication between wearable devices and a music player.
  • Blockchain & AI Make a Mark - On-demand streaming platforms integrate disruptive technology such as artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), analytics, cloud, IoT, and more to track the content usage pattern. The addition of features such as zero-buffering, cloud-based streaming service, large content repository, and Video-on-demand to support live content have simplified the use of OTT platforms. OTT media like Netflix, Hulu, and Amazon Prime depend on software like Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) to grab the attention of the users in the first 30 seconds of the interaction phase. Through AI and machine learning, OTT players can use Content-Aware Encoding (CAE) video compression to provide the best possible picture with minimal bandwidth consumption. With ML and AI, OTT media uses algorithms to analyze the most relevant content and experience for each user. Streaming on a blockchain is an emerging technology that enables content creators you to stream media without paying any fees. Blockchain live streaming is an emerging technology which allows content creators to upload content and get paid immediately, regardless of the device type. Blockchain-based video streaming app development has quickly become a competitive space with a number of start-ups entering the market, ranging from Livepeer to YouNow.
